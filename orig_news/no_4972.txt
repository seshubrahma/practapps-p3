Maeve Reston writes at CNN:  Democrats are spending the opening weeks of the Trump administration trying to flex their muscle any way they can  —   boycotting confirmation hearings, refusing to work with Republicans on Obamacare and pondering a filibuster of President Donald Trump’s Supreme Court pick. But as Democrats throw every procedural hurdle they can think of at Trump, they’re facing a bleak reality: they have virtually no power in Washington. The party has no clear successor to Barack Obama or Hillary Clinton who can speak with one voice for the party. And there is no consensus yet on a strategy to thwart Trump’s legislative agenda  —   or even how to prioritize the issues they plan to challenge him on. Read the rest of the story at CNN.
