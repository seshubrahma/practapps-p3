The river water was more corrosive and it wasn’t treated properly, so it ate into the old pipes and brought lead to the city’s residents, some of whom suffered health problems after this happened. The water supply has since been switched back to Lake Huron and the $87 million settlement resolves just one of the civil lawsuits that have been filed against state and city officials.   Some see it as a victory for the residents.   Others say it’s not enough and they’re moving out, if they haven’t already.
