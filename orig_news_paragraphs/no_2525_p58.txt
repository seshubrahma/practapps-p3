The most compelling argument against basic income is the most obvious: If everyone gets money without a requirement to do anything, humans may become morally depraved slackers. Jari Viljala finds this notion ridiculous. An electrician by trade, Mr. Viljala is accustomed to braving Arctic blasts of wind in    temperatures while threading wires into the spines of new housing complexes. He has left his wife and two daughters behind for as long as eight months at a time to venture north for construction projects.
