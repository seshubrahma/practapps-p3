 (CNN) President Donald Trump reportedly told former FBI Director James Comey, referring to the investigation into the activities of  former national security adviser Michael Flynn, that he hoped Comey would "let it go," according to a contemporaneous memo authored by Comey.    Since then, many have suggested that a president asking an FBI director to halt an investigation of a senior campaign adviser constitutes obstruction of justice, and is an impeachable offense. Obstruction of justice is a broadly defined federal crime.
