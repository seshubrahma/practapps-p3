Dr. Ibrahim  —   who heads the trauma unit at Orlando Regional Medical Center, and generally goes by Joey   —   had already spent hours repairing gunshot wounds in the bodies of Latino men and women, many of whom were the sons and daughters of immigrants, too. Yanked from bed by a phone call at 2:15 a. m. he was doing what he had trained for. His old friends from his hometown in Tennessee were pulling for him.
