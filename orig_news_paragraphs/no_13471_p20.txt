Phillips explained that in 1982 the Perry County’s district attorney’s office had "received several complaints from incumbent black candidates and black voters that absentee ballot applications were being mailed to citizens’ homes without their request. " Phillips said that their office performed an investigation and sought an indictment against Turner  —   empaneling a grand jury whose racial makeup was eleven blacks and seven whites. Phillips additionally testified that, in 1982, Turner was alleged to have engaged in illegal voting activities by picking up absentee ballots, even though he himself ran as a candidate in that election.
