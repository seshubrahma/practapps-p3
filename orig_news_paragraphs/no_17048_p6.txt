Just 16 years ago, George W. Bush lost the popular vote but squeezed out an Electoral College victory. Never before, though, has the Electoral College come to the rescue of a candidate who made it a central theme of his campaign that our politics are not only broken but corrupt. Never before have the distortionary effects of the Electoral College benefited a candidate who railed against an electoral system that, in his view, ignores the will of the people.   Again and again this past campaign, Donald Trump decried a political system that was "rigged.
