Accordingly, his administration is now aiming to dig and burn more coal from federal lands, force through controversial oil pipelines such as the Dakota Access and Keystone XL, relax fuel efficiency standards for cars and trucks, and revoke Obama’s Clean Power Plan. Partly these measures are symbolic, a sop to the deep Republican loathing of liberal environmentalism in America’s culture wars. Coal is no longer the cheapest option for electricity generation, and the clean energy revolution led by the combination of renewables and   fracked gas is beginning to look unstoppable.   But symbolism is important, particularly if China takes its cue from Trump’s America that the era of carbon controls is over.
