The recent brinkmanship between US President Donald Trump and Australian Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull over the fate of the refugee deal has attracted intense interest internationally.   So far, attention has focused largely on its ramifications for the deal and future relations between the two hitherto close allies, and the broader implications for US foreign policy not only on refugees and migration, but for relations with other States.   But what about the consequences for those people caught up in all of this     the 2, 500 children, women and men with heartbeats who have been marooned on Nauru and Manus Island for years?
