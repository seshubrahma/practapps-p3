 (CNN) President Barack Obama bade farewell to G20 leaders in Hangzhou, China, on Monday by reminding them they’re living in "turbulent" times    and he wasn’t kidding.   His valedictory Asia tour, which moved on to Laos later in the day, is unfolding amid diplomatic slights and great power rivalries that reflect the unstable nature of the world Obama will bequeath to his successor in January. New players, The controversies reflect the way international politics is now a stew of many competing, rising or resurgent powers that see fewer reasons to simply fall into line behind the United States than was the case following World War II and the Cold War.
