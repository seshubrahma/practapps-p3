This type of political flexibility is not altogether unheard of in the nation’s capital. Back in 2011, Josh Rosner, who advocated for preserving Fannie and Freddie to preserve minority homeowners in 2016 and decried the alleged use of their funds to support Obamacare in 2017, appeared before a House oversight panel alongside Dr. Anthony Sanders, a George Mason University finance professor. " I really want to go with what the administration said earlier, which is to start paring them down if not dismantling them," Sanders told the panel after arguing that Fannie and Freddie’s guarantees were keeping private capital out of the mortgage market.
