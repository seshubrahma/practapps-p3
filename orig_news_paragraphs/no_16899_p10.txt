The word has long permeated music from the tongues of distinguished artists. Jazz great Jelly Roll Morton said he sang smutty songs about women to appear tough because he played piano, which was considered to be a women’s instrument at the time. Elton John and the Rolling Stones called on bitches in ’70s rock anthems. Grandfathers of hip hop Public Enemy and N. W. A employed "bitch" to craft their hard, streetwise personas with songs like "Sophisticated Bitch" and "Bitch Iz a Bitch.
