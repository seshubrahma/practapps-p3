 (CNN) The US Army Corps of Engineers has granted an easement in North Dakota for the controversial Dakota Access Pipeline, allowing the project to move toward completion despite the protests of Native Americans and environmentalists.   "With this action, Dakota Access now has received all federal authorizations necessary to proceed expeditiously to complete construction of the pipeline," said Energy Transfer Partners, the parent firm of the company building the pipeline.   Vicki Granado, spokeswoman for the Energy Transfer Partners, said work would start "immediately.
