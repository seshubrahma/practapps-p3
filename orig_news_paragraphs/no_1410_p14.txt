Roy M. Neel, who was a campaign manager for Al Gore in his 1992   run, said in an oral history of the Bill Clinton years that women in the South particularly disliked Mrs. Clinton, the first working mother to serve as first lady and the first  —   and, so far, the only  —   to have an office in the West Wing, because she "appeared to be something of an affront to their sense of who they were.
