North Carolina may be the most evenly divided presidential battleground in the country. Its two biggest population centers, Charlotte and the   Research Triangle of Raleigh, Durham and Chapel Hill, have been transformed by an influx of political centrists from other states. The   party registration preference is not Republican nor Democrat, but unaffiliated. The rural white "Jessecrats," conservative Democrats who reliably cast ballots for Mr. Helms, are dying off.
