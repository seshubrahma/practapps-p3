For many, it heralded a fulfillment of the   republicanism on which they believed the country had itself been founded in its colonial revolt against unfair taxation without representation. The salient combination of   continental nationalism, and Lord Protector of institutions has been revived at times, most recently by Donald Trump. That he is wealthy is irrelevant —  many populists, from Jackson to progressive James Weaver —  were wealthy. The key feature of these populists is that they appear concerned with America and not politics, which many cynical rural voters believe to be mutually exclusive.
