The public was with Clinton, convinced that Republicans had overreached, and Clinton’s foes were the ones who suffered the most political damage.    Some scandals with clear evidence of wrongdoing bring down officials in the White House    without direct risk to the president himself. Watergate spawned the phrase, "What did the president know and when did he know it?"    and that has become a question that often determines how much damage a scandal can cause a president.
