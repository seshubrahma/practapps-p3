The Democrats’ regulatory regime gave the U. S. government the effective right to take control of the Internet, create new taxation authority, and regulate the "fairness" of political thought under rules that once applied to the old ATT telephone monopoly. Under federal administrative law, the   FCC rule change allows a   initial public comment until July 17, and then another 30 days for replies to those comments by Aug. 16. Advocates of Net Neutrality called it a democratizing principle. But opponents complained that under Net Neutrality, corporate interests like Amazon, Netflix and Google’s YouTube could make huge profits by clogging cable delivery pipes with enormous amounts of data at no cost.
