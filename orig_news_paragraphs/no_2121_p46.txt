Some critics are wary of expanding private investment in public infrastructure. Although cities may get cash up front in these deals, "there is no ‘free’ money" in   partnerships, says a 2008 Government Accountability Office report. Using roads as an example, the report observed "it is likely" that tolls will increase more on a privately operated highway than one run by the government. Ms. Matson, of Table Rock, who has attended White House meetings on infrastructure, has tried to dispel concerns about these deals.
