In the last two and a half years, officials say, they have issued visas to more than 8, 000 Afghans and their immediate families through the program, which members of the armed services, military officials and lawmakers from both parties have hailed as indispensable to national security. Congress has responded to the State Department’s program parsimoniously, allocating the special visas piecemeal through its annual defense policy bill.
