Still it’s hard to miss the symbolism of Mrs. Reagan’s passing —   she died Sunday at age 94 —  coinciding with the final collapse of the Republican ruling class as GOP voters over the weekend anointed one of two antiestablishment outsiders, Donald Trump and Ted Cruz, as their likely new standard bearer. With her death and the eclipsing of the GOP establishment, there is a palpable sense that something in the Grand Old Party —  to which Nancy and Ronald Reagan devoted decades of their lives —  has been lost forever. There is more than a little irony to that outcome.
