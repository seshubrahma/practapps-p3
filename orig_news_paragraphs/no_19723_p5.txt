Historically, creativity has been portrayed as a mysterious, elusive force —  a gift from the gods or the muses. Creativity can’t be summoned, the thinking goes, let alone taught to the mentally inflexible, unimaginative,   masses. Design thinking upends that perception and assumes that anyone can be a creative  . At its best, design thinking incorporates   teaching techniques such as   inquiry and collaborative   and dovetails nicely with   learning curricula that emphasize interpersonal skills such as collaboration and empathy.
