NAFTA suddenly opened unlimited access for American companies to Mexico’s formerly protected consumer and industrial markets. The ensuing U. S. export boom saw America’s balance of payments surplus with Mexico spike by over 1, 000 percent, to $29. 4 billion in 1994. The resulting capital flight caused a massive credit crunch and forced Mexico’s central bank to raise interest rates dramatically. Mexican banks began to collapse as borrowers could no longer pay loans.
