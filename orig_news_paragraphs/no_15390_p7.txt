His determination to push through   liberal reforms made him unpopular on the government’s own benches. With a backbench rebellion and government defeat looming, the   "Macron Law," which aimed to shake up the economy through labor reform, had to be forced through the National Assembly with the help of a controversial parliamentary measure. It led to several days of protest, but also to Macron’s realization that it was not just the economy that needed to change, but the system itself.
