Both Politico and Bloomberg reported Monday that the White House is reevaluating Spicer’s role. The shakeup would cap off Spicer’s tumultuous   tenure at the White House podium, which made him not only one of the administration’s most prominent personalities, but also the face of the White House’s unfocused messaging strategy. " We have sought input from many people as we look to expand our communications operation," White House deputy press secretary Sarah Huckabee Sanders said in a statement.
