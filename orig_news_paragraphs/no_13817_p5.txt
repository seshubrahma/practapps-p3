Before Barack Obama even stepped foot in the Oval Office, Republican leaders vowed to oppose much of his agenda. But when President Obama nominated Eric Holder for attorney general, Republicans gave him a vote and he was confirmed on February 2.    The Senate confirmed Hillary Clinton as secretary of state on January 21, the day after President Obama took the oath of office.   Republicans could have pulled any number of parliamentary tricks to delay the confirmation of two people whom they had every reason to oppose.
