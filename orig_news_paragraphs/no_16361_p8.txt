But Bible scholars have recently added color to our picture of the stranger by looking more closely at his past. Most often, Frank Spina argues, the stranger left his place of origin on account of "social and political upheaval due to war, famine, economic and social troubles, oppression, plague and other misfortunes that produced strife.
