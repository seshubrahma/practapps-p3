Trump’s strong admiration for Russian President Vladimir Putin and interesting in getting the relationship with Russia back on track led many to assume that he would do little to interfere in Syria, where Moscow is closely allied with Damascus. This was reinforced by Secretary of State Rex Tillerson’s comment in March that it would be "up to the Syrian people" whether or not Assad would go, a demand long made by the Obama administration. Regime change, it seemed, was no longer desirable for the US. Yet, within a few weeks, everything changed.
