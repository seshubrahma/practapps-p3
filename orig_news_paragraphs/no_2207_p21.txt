The names of the victims may change, but the Greek Chorus reaction is all too familiar. Shock and grief, candlelight vigils and calls for unity, vows for change and legislative paralysis,   and vitriol, and, in the end, nothing much different   —   other than, say, South Carolina’s vote to remove the Confederate flag from State House grounds after the Charleston shooting. It took a   filibuster by Senator Chris Murphy, a Democrat from Connecticut with searing memories of the slaughter of 26 schoolchildren and educators in Newtown, to get modest   measures to the Senate floor.
