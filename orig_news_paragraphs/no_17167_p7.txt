Unlike my friend Eli Pariser, who places blame on unaccountable algorithms for "filter bubbles," I see these echo chambers as the result of the basic human drive toward homophily, the tendency of birds of a feather to flock together.   The Internet has allowed very different flocks to form than in analog days. In the days of three television networks, a relatively narrow range of political views were discussed in public fora.
