Now it’s worth pointing out that Lincoln actually had been a lawyer for the railroads back in Illinois in the 1850s.   Moreover, by his own bootstrapping nature, he was a strong believer in private property and free enterprise.   However, he saw something crucial: There had to be a balance between capital and labor, and the government —  hopefully, a Republican government —   had to calibrate that balance, always making sure that the interests of workers and small landholders were protected.
