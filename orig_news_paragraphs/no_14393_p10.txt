But the Court made clear that while the President deserves deference in this area, he is not entitled to imperial rule. And while the President’s emissaries made the argument that he is privy to information that no one else has the Court countered that he could have shared it with them under seal to ensure that it be kept secret —   as custom dictates. That, however, did not happen.   Without any concrete basis in which to justify the President’s Executive order, the Court struggled to see how it could be considered necessary.
