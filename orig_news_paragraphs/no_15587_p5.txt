In 1790, Congress created a federal misprision statute that is substantially similar to its descendant today (18 U. S. C. § 4) —  except the modern statute criminalizes concealment, not silence.   Mere silence, without some affirmative act, is insufficient evidence of the crime of misprision of felony.    Misprision of a felony occurs when: (1) a third party commits an alleged felony and (2) the defendant has full knowledge of the crime
