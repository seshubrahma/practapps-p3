Other recent polling, conducted before Sanders’ endorsement of Clinton and after the announcement that she would not face charges for her use of private email, found a tightening race, with independents shifting away from Clinton. In this poll, independent voters were more apt to back her in the   matchup than they were last June, but in a   contest, her support among independents is steady. Clinton’s continued lead may be due to the advantages she holds over Trump in likability (43% of voters nationally have a favorable view of Clinton vs. 39% who view Trump positively) experience (64% say she has the right experience to be president, double the 32% who say so about Trump) and on a range of issues including foreign policy, health care, abortion and race relations.
