Inside the White House, the temporary suspension of the limits for parts of Yemen and Somalia is seen as a test run while the government considers whether to more broadly rescind or relax the   rules, said the officials, who described the internal deliberations on the condition of anonymity. The move to open the throttle on using military force   —   and accept a greater risk of civilian casualties   —   in troubled parts of the Muslim world comes as the Trump administration is also trying to significantly increase military spending and cut foreign aid and State Department budgets.
