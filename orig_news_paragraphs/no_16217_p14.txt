Trump’s acceptance of the Republican nomination represents a stunning moment in American political history   —   as the outsider who overturned the Republican establishment leaves Cleveland with his sights firmly on the White House. His address barely touched on the issue of Republican disunity that plagued much of the 2016 convention    culminating in Cruz’s dramatic speech Wednesday. Instead, he outlined a broader and loftier argument to millions of Americans watching at home, seizing his best chance yet to present himself as possessed of the qualities and sobriety demanded of a President.
