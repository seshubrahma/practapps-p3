Along the   border (CNN) Marisela Ramirez’s hands tremble as she rubs them together to control the shaking. The dirt under her fingernails hints at the hardscrabble journey she’s endured since leaving her home in southern Mexico weeks ago. Ramirez is in Nogales on the Mexican side of the border with the US     sitting in a shelter eating breakfast    no more than 100 yards from an Arizona town bearing the same name.
