The 9th Circuit concedes they should generally defer to the other branches on matters of immigration and national security.   But that doesn’t mean that courts lack the authority to review the constitutionality of executive actions.   It’s a huge statement on the separation of powers, and it’s not necessarily the last word on the matter.    The 9th Circuit’s opinion on this issue is not a final determination on the merits this decision just keeps the temporary restraining order from the district court in place.
