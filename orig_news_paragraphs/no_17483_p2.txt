Still, trouble abounded. Often, the contractors weren’t as well trained they weren’t as sensitive to the communities they were policing and they weren’t as scrupulous when it came to the laws, practices, and customs of civilized military engagement.   It is therefore not surprising that allegations of criminal and civil misconduct attached to many of the biggest contractors and that the   governments in Kabul and Baghdad regularly pleaded with the State Department to rein these contractors in.
