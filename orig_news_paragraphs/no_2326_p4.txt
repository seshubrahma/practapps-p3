In the birther movement, Mr. Trump recognized an opportunity to connect with the electorate over an issue many considered taboo: the discomfort, in some quarters of American society, with the election of the nation’s first black president. He harnessed it for political gain, beginning his connection with the largely white Republican base that, in his 2016 campaign, helped clinch his party’s nomination. " The appeal of the birther issue was, ‘I’m going to take this guy on, and I’m going to beat him,’" said Sam Nunberg, who was one of Mr. Trump’s advisers during that period but was fired from his current campaign.
