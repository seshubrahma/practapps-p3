After a few months in an Atlanta halfway house, Ronald Moon is now a free man.   An unsustainable system, Revisiting the sentences of inmates like Moon has been a second term priority for the Obama administration. According to a bipartisan task force report released earlier this year, the population in federal prisons has ballooned seven times in the past 36 years, with nearly 200, 000 incarcerated. Much of that growth is the result of mandatory minimum sentences for drug convictions, a consequence mirrored in prison costs that now consume nearly   of the overall Department of Justice budget.
