As a judge on the 10th U. S. Circuit Court of Appeals since 2006, Gorsuch has narrowly interpreted constitutional rights and statutory benefits. He takes an "originalist" approach, reading the Constitution in terms of its 18th Century understanding. Separately, the Harvard law graduate who studied at the University of Oxford, wrote a book, "The Future of Assisted Suicide and Euthanasia," that argues against medical aid in dying and deems the intentional taking of human life by private persons "always wrong.
