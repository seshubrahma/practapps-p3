It hangs chockablock with the numerous other   items in open storage on the American Wing mezzanine, dim behind thick plexiglass and hard to find. It’s a good picture, of a young woman, probably MacDowell’s sister Elizabeth, absorbed in a book not, in other words, just sitting there blank and staring, but focused,   occupied, the way the reading, talking and listening women in Mary Cassatt’s paintings are, and the way Gertrude Stein is, just by being there, a human magnet, in Picasso’s marmoreal   portrait in Gallery 911 of the Modern and Contemporary Wing. " Picasso never painted another woman like that," the New York artist Deborah Kass once said about this work, "who looked like that, with that kind of presence, who wasn’t a thing!
