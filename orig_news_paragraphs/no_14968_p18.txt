While Tillerson noted Turkey had suffered attacks at the hands of PKK, he did not equate the group with the YPG.   Kurds aren’t the only source of friction in the US relationship with Turkey, a NATO ally that hosts two US military bases. Key among them is that the US has yet to hand over the Turkish cleric Fethullah Gulen, a US resident whom Erdogan accuses of orchestrating a coup attempt last summer. Turkey’s demand that Washington extradite Gulen, whom Turkey blames for leading last year’s failed coup, loomed over Tillerson’s talks.
