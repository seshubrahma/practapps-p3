American jihadists are generally motivated by a mix of factors, including dislike of U. S. foreign policy in the Muslim world. For many, embracing the ideology of Osama bin Laden or ISIS allowed them to become the heroes of their own story as well as actors in a cosmic crusade. For others a "cognitive opening" to militant Islam, was often precipitated by a personal disappointment or loss. For each individual terrorist the proportion of these motivations varied.
