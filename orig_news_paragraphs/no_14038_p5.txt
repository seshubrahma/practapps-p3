In a statement to the Department of Defense Friday night, Mattis pointedly praised the intelligence community, saying that along with the Pentagon, they are "the sentinels and guardians of our nation. " He also vowed to "work with the State Department to strengthen our alliances."   The comments were notable in their contrast to public positions taken by Trump, most notably his skepticism of the intelligence community’s increasing belief that Russia meddled in the 2016 election to aid him and hurt Hillary Clinton’s campaign.
