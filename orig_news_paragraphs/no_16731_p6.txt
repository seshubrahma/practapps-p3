He may have been reaching toward his car window, but why would that necessarily create an immediate threat to these officers or anyone else? I suppose it is odd that he was n’t following the officer’s commands, but that by itself doesn’t mean he posed a danger to them.   An officer can’t shoot someone just because the person won’t do what the officer asks him to do, but is empowered to use excessive force based on a reasonable belief that the person poses an immediate threat of serious injury to the officer or someone else.
