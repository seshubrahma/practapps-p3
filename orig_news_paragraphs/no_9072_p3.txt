Xiaoping Ren has been called "China’s Frankenstein," though it’s not a moniker he’d claim for himself. His office displays a bronzed pig’s ear gifted to him by a team he helped to switch pigs’ forelegs. It was those experiments that prepared him to be a part of the first successful transplant of a human hand. His colleague, Sergio Canavero, is the one who embraces the mad scientist references.
