House Speaker Paul Ryan backed the expansion in December 2015. Growing public and media criticism prompted Ryan to withdrew his support in 2016, so allowing the program to drop back down to 66, 000 visas per year level.   The   program is the   version of the   visa outsourcing program, which allows a population of roughly 650, 000   foreign university graduates to take jobs sought by young American college grads. The   program gets a lot of bad publicity, partly because it threatens the peers and children of influential   professionals in the computer, academic, healthcare and business sectors, who also have ready access to sympathetic journalists.
