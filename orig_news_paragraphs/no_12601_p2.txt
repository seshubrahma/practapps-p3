As well as domestic plays like ‘Bremain’ and ‘Bremorse’ the phrase has also set the style for European freedom movements, with names like ‘Frexit’ for France and ‘Nexit’ for the Netherlands. The dictionary reports the first usage of Brexit was 2013, with a 3, 400 per cent jump in usage just this year  —   meaning it has been a long road to acceptance by the dictionary establishment for the term.
