The laws of power apply, and those with influence find it easiest to use social media to get even more. The smartphone camera is not a de facto tool of democracy. It just magnifies whatever it is turned on    and strongmen like Erdogan and Syrian President Bashar   are learning to use that immediacy to their advantage. The design of the iPhone tells us how FaceTime conversations should be conducted: we cradle the phone a few inches from our face, bringing us into close connection with someone else doing precisely the same thing.
