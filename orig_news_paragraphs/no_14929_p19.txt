Startling decline in Americans’ life expectancy, Comparing American survival to that of other nations can be startling. The United States ranks 37th in the World Health Organization’s annual health systems assessment, behind not only the   societies like Japan, and the entire continent of Europe, but also below tiny Costa Rica. With a GDP per capita, in 2015, of only $9, 200, Costa Ricans live an average one year longer  than their far wealthier US counterparts. US male mortality is 18% higher than in Costa Rica American female mortality is 10% higher than Costa Ricans for women under 65 years of age.
