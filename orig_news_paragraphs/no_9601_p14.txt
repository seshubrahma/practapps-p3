Although Emmons, as a contractor, was not formally displaced and was eventually able to land on his feet, he watched as some of his friends —  unable to find new employment —  were forced into early retirement. Others, far too young to retire, were forced to abandon their trade and leave the tech industry altogether —  even though that meant letting years of specialized schooling and experience in the   sector go to waste. For many of those workers, their last opportunity to use the specialized skills they had spent decades cultivating was when they had to "transfer" that career’s worth of knowledge to their   foreign replacements, who would work for a fraction of the cost. Emmons recalled one coworker, who after twenty years in the IT industry, was forced to go into landscaping to make ends meet.
