I’ve talked so much about being a grandmother. Now I’m going to be talking doubly about being a grandmother," Clinton crooned at a campaign rally in Columbus, Ohio, last week, as she boasted about the birth of her second grandchild.   The constant reminder to voters that she is a woman is a notable contrast from the way Clinton approached the issue eight years ago. Back then, the   senator and her team viewed her gender as a potential liability that could undercut her efforts to exude strength and experience in a field crowded with men.
