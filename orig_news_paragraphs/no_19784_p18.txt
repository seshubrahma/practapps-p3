It is an adage in   policy that policy for poor people makes for poor policy. The more people who are included in the   system,   argues, the more likely it is to succeed, because with them comes voting power. This is where economics meets politics.   Although it might be more economically rational to dedicate all the resources of the Excelsior Scholarship Program to   students, the political reality is that they might enjoy a greater benefit if wealthier New Yorkers also have a personal stake in the program.
