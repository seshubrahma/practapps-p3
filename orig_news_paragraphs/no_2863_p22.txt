In recent weeks, China changed course. It halted most coal trading on commodities markets and encouraged   mines to sign   contracts at low prices with power stations. This month, the National Development and Reform Commission raised the number of days that mines could operate to 330 days per year. China will most likely be able to avoid blackouts, said Chang Yijun, the president of Shanxi Fenwei Energy, a regional coal consulting firm, who added that remaining output caps like the   rule would still limit growth in emissions of greenhouse gases.
