Even after Ronald Reagan’s   landslide in 1980, we paid little attention to the right: The central narrative of America’s political development was still believed to be the rise of the liberal state. But as Newt Gingrich’s   revolutionaries prepared to take over the House of Representatives in 1994, the scholar Alan Brinkley published an essay called "The Problem of American Conservatism" in The American Historical Review. American conservatism, Brinkley argued, "had been something of an orphan in historical scholarship," and that was "coming to seem an    omission. "
