While Trump has continually denied any collusion and has lobbed accusations of his own, the FBI appears to have been keeping its eye on the ball. President Trump’s decision to fire Comey is the second such dismissal to rock the administration. When the President announced the resignation of Acting Attorney General Sally Yates in January for refusing to implement the administration’s refugee ban, the comparison to President Richard Nixon’s "Saturday Night Massacre" instantly lit up the headlines.   In that 1973 event, Nixon fired independent prosecutor Archibald Cox for his aggressive inquiry into Watergate.
