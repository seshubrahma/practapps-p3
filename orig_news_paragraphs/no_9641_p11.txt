And freedom of worship, too, is under fire, as Christians have learned to their dismay. In addition, we all know that freedom from fear is bitter joke to many Americans, because of domestic crime and unrest, or because of foolish foreign wars, or because of the surging threat from domestic terrorism —  even as our own government insists on importing, at taxpayer expense, more possible terrorists. And the fourth of FDR’s freedoms, "freedom from want," also still raises hackles. Many libertarians, for example, inveigh against freedom from want as not a freedom at all, but rather as a threat —  the threat of coercive taxation.
