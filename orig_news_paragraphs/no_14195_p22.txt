" The petitioners have a strong likelihood of success in establishing that the removal of the petitioner and others similarly situated violates their due process and equal protection guaranteed by the United States Constitution," US District Judge Ann Donnelly wrote in her decision.   " There is imminent danger that, absent the stay of removal, there will be substantial and irreparable injury to refugees,   and other individuals from nations subject to the January 27, 2017, Executive Order," the ruling said.
