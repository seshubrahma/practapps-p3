In June 1941, it was just a field, 30 miles outside of Detroit.   And yet by September of the the following year, it was producing   Liberator bombers, each with 488, 193 parts, capable of delivering four tons of ordnance on a target nearly three thousand miles.   By 1945, Willow Run had produced nearly 9, 000 of these fearsome birds of war.   And oh, by the way, it employed 42, 000 men, and women, at its peak —  including Rosie the Riveter (even if Rosie’s story is, shall we say, clouded by legend).
