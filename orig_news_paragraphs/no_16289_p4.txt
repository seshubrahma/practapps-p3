The trip has also been a chance for Clinton to cut into Republican’s claim that he is the change candidate in the 2016 race. It is an easy case for him to make: Democrats have controlled the White House for eight years and Trump, a political novice, regularly talks about how he would bring a different set of skills to the White House. Republicans have even used the status quo argument against Clinton. Mike Pence, Trump’s vice presidential nominee, called Clinton the "secretary of status quo" in his speech at the Republican National Convention earlier this month.
