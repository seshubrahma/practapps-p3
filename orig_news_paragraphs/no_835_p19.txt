Americans for Prosperity, which has spent tens of millions of dollars in advertising opposing the Affordable Care Act, is bringing back some of the subjects of its initial ads  —   ordinary people who spoke about the problems with "  medicine"  —   for its new push. And it is arming activists with supporting evidence   —    it found, for instance, that 98 percent of Republicans in the Senate, or 51 of the 52, had voted at one point to repeal the law.
