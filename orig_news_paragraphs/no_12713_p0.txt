In a new survey, a   organization called PRRI put an interesting spin on the traditional "right track, wrong track" polling question by asking if "American culture and way of life have changed for the worse or better since the 1950s. " It turns out a slim majority think the Fifties were better, by 51% to 48%. [This is a new angle because pollsters usually ask if the country is on the "wrong track" without giving a specific example of what the "right track" would look like.
