What does Trump’s focus on specific companies —  and often on relatively small decisions those companies make —  mean for American society, and specifically for American business? Under Trump, many companies will (and already do) feel pulled two ways —  in one direction towards their interests in capturing the economic efficiencies of globalization and in the other direction toward appeasing a U. S. president whose outbursts and obloquies are unpredictable and bad for their business, and who has a personal stake in scoring political wins by keeping manufacturing domestic, efficiency be damned. From his behavior so far, it seems that Trump thinks a big part of his job will be to examine and adjudicate particular companies’ behavior and to decide what’s allowed and what’s fair on a situational basis, not just on a policy one.
