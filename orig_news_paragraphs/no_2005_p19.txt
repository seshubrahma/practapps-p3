In the end, heavy opposition in the south and other regions marred by high unemployment levels proved insurmountable, and Mr. Renzi could not persuade enough moderates to stick with him and his government’s plan to modernize Italy. Ultimately, the substance of the reform mattered less than voters’ opinions of Mr. Renzi.
