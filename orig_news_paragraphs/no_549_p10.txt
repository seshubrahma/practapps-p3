Keenly aware of the "justifiable international public interest," the editor quickly wrote and published an article about the Kuwaiti ban without realizing that it was based on "a decision made in 2011," according to a statement posted on the site on Tuesday after The New York Times inquired about the article’s origin. By the time the error was spotted and the article updated, the first, erroneous version had already spread. " Mistakes happen and unfortunately we found ourselves in a situation where a serious mistake like this (for which the member of staff has been disciplined) went viral," the site’s statement said.
