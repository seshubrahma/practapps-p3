Easing pollution restrictions could delay the closing of some old   power plants, slowing the switch by some utilities to other sources. And with the government pendulum swinging from environmental concerns back to job creation and energy independence, share prices of many energy companies, particularly coal producers, soared Tuesday on the news. For coal executives, however, optimism and expansion plans remain guarded. Regulatory relief could restore 10 percent of their companies’ lost market share at most, they say  —   nowhere near enough to return coal to its dominant position in power markets and put tens of thousands of coal miners to work.
