The centerpiece of Ford’s investment package involves spending $850 million to upgrade its assembly plant in Wayne, Mich. to build a new Ranger pickup and Bronco S. U. V. The decision to add the new models was previously agreed to in Ford’s labor contract with the United Automobile Workers, and was hardly a surprise given the industry’s continued shift toward larger, more utilitarian vehicles. And although Mr. Trump basked in the news with a Twitter message that hailed "car companies coming back to U. S.," Ford said the decision was prompted by the realities of the current marketplace.
