The poll was conducted over three days, March 14 to March 16, during two days of which Sen. Marco Rubio ( ) was still in the race. Rubio dropped out on the evening of March 15 after an abysmal performance in his home state of Florida, where Trump walloped him by more than double digits. In the portion of the poll conducted before Rubio suspended his campaign, the   Floridian got just four percent in New York. Nineteen percent chose someone else or were undecided.
