As the movement grew larger and more popular, public support for granting voting rights to educated white women became strong. But the majority of the American public and opponents of woman suffrage did not believe "other" women    immigrants and workers, Chinese women in California, Jewish radicals in New York and   women everywhere    deserved the vote. Leaders of mainstream suffrage organizations kept these "others" out of sight for, they would say, "tactical" reasons.             The result is those suffrage tableaus that keep popping up in the media today.
