Washington (CNN) Donald Trump and Hillary Clinton are neck and neck in North Carolina, a state solidifying its position as a perennial presidential election battleground, while Trump holds a   lead in the traditionally   state of Arizona, according to new   battleground state polls.   Trump is the choice of 43% of registered voters in Arizona, while Clinton stands at 38% followed by Libertarian candidate Gary Johnson at 12% and Green Party nominee Jill Stein at 4%. In North Carolina, Clinton stands at 44% Trump at 43% and Johnson at 11%.
