Medicare uses a guideline of mammography testing every one to two years for women age 40 years and older, according to the 2002 standard set forth by the US Preventive Services Task Force, an independent, volunteer panel of national experts in prevention and   medicine. (The task force has proposed a more recent guideline, which has not yet been adopted by Medicare.) When it comes to colonoscopy, the task force recommendation is to get tested starting at age 50 and continuing until 75.
