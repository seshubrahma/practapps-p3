Since the election, mortgage rates have climbed roughly half a percentage point to a   high, adding hundreds, sometimes thousands, of dollars to a home buyer’s yearly payments. (The annual cost of a $400, 000 mortgage, for example, rose almost $700.) The speed and size of the increase took many lenders and borrowers by surprise   —   and the increase is expected to reverberate across the housing industry, particularly if rates continue to rise next year.
