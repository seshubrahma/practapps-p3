She also unveiled two new policies to protect workers, including curbing the use of "forced arbitration" clauses in contracts that prohibit workers and consumers from bringing legal action against companies that have harmed them. " We are not going to let corporations like Wells Fargo use these   ‘gotchas’ to escape accountability," Mrs. Clinton said. And she took aim at pharmaceutical companies, against the backdrop of outrage against Mylan, which repeatedly raised the price of EpiPens, leading to widespread consumer anger, particularly among parents whose children rely on the lifesaving injections after allergic reactions.
