In March 1926, "Lolly Willowes" by the British author Sylvia Townsend Warner was gaining acclaim, and a month later it became the club’s inaugural pick. Famously, a panel of literary experts made the choices over lunch and sherry around an oak table. Their credibility built the fledgling club’s membership. They had hits like "Gone With the Wind" and "The Catcher in the Rye.
