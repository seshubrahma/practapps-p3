The impact could reverberate throughout the valley’s precarious economy, where agriculture is by far the largest industry. With 6. 5 million people living in the valley, the fields in this state bring in $35 billion a year and provide more of the nation’s food than any other state. The consequences of a smaller immigrant work force would ripple not just through the orchards and dairies, but also to locally owned businesses, restaurants, schools and even seemingly unrelated industries, like the insurance market. Many here feel vindicated by the election, and signs declaring "Vote to make America great again" still dot the highways.
