Bridges, tunnels, ports, dams, pipelines, and airfields now have geopolitical clout that rivals democratically elected civic institutions. Infrastructure is now conflict —  and cooperation —  by other means. Architect and Yale University urbanist Keller Easterling refers to this as the practice of extrastatecraft, or an emerging form of power, manipulation, and influence that exists outside the norms of what we currently consider representative government ( e. g. #bridgegate).
