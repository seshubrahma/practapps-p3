If the    labor policy was ended, employers would soon run out of Americans to hire, and would then start offering higher wages   —   and training   —   to recruit some of the 7 million American   men who have given up working. That’s how salaries for   Americans rose briefly in the boom years of 1998 and 1999, and why the auto companies recruited so many southern African Americans during the 1940s, 1950s, and 1960s.
