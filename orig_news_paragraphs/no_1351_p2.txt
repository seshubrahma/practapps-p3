Mr. Cruz can take little solace from his vaunted   operation even if he prevails there. It turns out that delegates  —   like ordinary voters   —   are susceptible to shifts in public opinion. And as the gravitational pull of Mr. Trump’s recent primary landslides draws more Republicans toward him, Mr. Cruz’s support among the party’s 2, 472 convention delegates is softening, threatening his hopes of preventing Mr. Trump’s nomination by overtaking him in a floor fight.
