CBS’s Edward R. Murrow, one of the greatest journalists of the 1940s and 1950s, famously took on Sen. Joseph McCarthy,  who was throwing around all sorts of accusations about Americans he claimed allegedly working for the communists. In 1954, using McCarthy’s own words against him, Murrow went on the air to expose the senator’s lies and contradictions. Washington Post reporters Carl Bernstein and Bob Woodward became legends in the   for their work uncovering the Watergate scandal and the myriad ways the Nixon administration broke laws.
