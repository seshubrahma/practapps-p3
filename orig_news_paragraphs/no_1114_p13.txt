By finding gaps in the technological skill of famous hackers, the creators of these zines justified the release of the private information the hackers had failed to protect. Now, as information security has become increasingly central to both global politics and personal relationships, "owned" has migrated from the online underground to the mainstream. There has never been a stronger relationship between who we are perceived to be and what kind of information we have. Recent concerns over "filter bubbles," "fake news" and political memes churned out by Russian trolls lay bare the fact that our beliefs are controlled by the data we consume.
