And as we shall see, the theoretical and the applied can inhabit, at different times, the same person. Thomas Jefferson: Theoretical Libertarian and Applied Libertarian, To illustrate the dichotomy, we can think back on the life of Thomas Jefferson, patron saint of American libertarianism. Yet interestingly, Jefferson’s reputation as a libertarian icon is based mostly on the words and deeds in the first part of his long life —  in the 1770s, 1780s, and early 1790s.   In the first of those epochal decades, of course, Jefferson wrote the Declaration of Independence, which stands for all time as a milestone in human freedom.
