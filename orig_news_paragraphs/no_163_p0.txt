American novelists have long complained about the ability of real life to outstrip fiction. In his landmark 1961 essay "Writing American Fiction," Philip Roth observed that "actuality is continually outdoing our talents, and the culture tosses up figures almost daily that are the envy of any novelist. " The figure Roth cites is Charles Van Doren, of   scandal fame but place Mr. Van Doren next to Donald J. Trump, and you can measure the change in the nature of credibility over the past  .
