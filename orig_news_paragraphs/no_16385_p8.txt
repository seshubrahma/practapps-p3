The big question now is whether this distinct social democratic tilt will prevent Democrats from taking full advantage of the GOP’s blunder in making Trump their standard bearer.   The conventional view among party elites, echoed by political reporters, is that Clinton must keep railing against the "rigged game," free trade, Wall Street and other populist bogeymen, for two reasons. One is to prevent Trump from making deeper inroads among white,   voters in Midwestern swing states like Ohio and Wisconsin. The other is to keep Sanders’ voters    especially his young white supporters    in the fold.
