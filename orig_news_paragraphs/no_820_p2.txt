One month in, an M. I. T. Media Lab analysis confirmed that Trump supporters "exist in their own information bubble," as Vice reported   —   and that journalists didn’t let Trump supporters into their bubbles, either. The filter bubble describes the tendency of social networks like Facebook and Twitter to lock users into personalized feedback loops, each with its own news sources, cultural touchstones and political inclinations. We seem to like these places, and so do social media companies
