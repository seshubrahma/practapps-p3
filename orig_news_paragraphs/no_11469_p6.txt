Because the contested delegates in the Democratic primary in California are allocated on a proportional basis within each congressional district, and some districts have more delegates than others, it makes more sense for Clinton and Sanders to concentrate on districts where they are already strongest. In contrast, because Republican delegates are awarded on a    basis, with three delegates in each district, candidates must venture beyond their "safe" seats to win.
