What we are witnessing on both sides of the Atlantic is a steady slip toward division and change for the sake of change. But for all the talk around Trump’s presidency, it is in Europe where this decade’s most profound political changes could occur. Elections in the Netherlands on Wednesday and in France next month risk dismantling a world order that has seen peace in Europe for nearly three generations.   In Holland, Geert Wilders and his Freedom party are surfing a wave of   sentiment, buoyed by dissatisfaction with traditional political parties.
