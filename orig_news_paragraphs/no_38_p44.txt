He remembers checking his email at 4 a. m. for messages from East Coast associates. Without thinking much about the notification, he clicked on the "change password" button and half asleep, as best he can remember, he typed in a new password. What he did not know until months later is that he had just given the Russian hackers access to his email account. Hundreds of similar phishing emails were being sent to American political targets, including an identical email sent on March 19 to Mr. Podesta, chairman of the Clinton campaign.
