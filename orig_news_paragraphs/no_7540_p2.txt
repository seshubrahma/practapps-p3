The deal requires Iran to slow its nuclear enrichment activities for several years, in exchange for relief from international sanctions. Critics say  —   and President Barack Obama has admitted  —   that even if Iran keeps the deal, it will still be able to produce nuclear weapons after a decade or so. Iran also claims that the deal allows it to continue developing   ballistic missiles, which the Obama administration had promised it would not.
