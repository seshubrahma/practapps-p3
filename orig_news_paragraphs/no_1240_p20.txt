A pilot radioed the coordinates of the exfiltration point and the unit started the long trek back to the choppers in humidity so thick it felt as if they could slice through it with their hunting knives. Drenched in sweat as they boarded the helicopters, the squad could see the smoke billowing from the destroyed vehicles, a small victory in the battle against deforestation. " I never dreamed that I would hold a rifle in my hands to defend the Amazon," said one    GEF member, a former environmental activist who declined to disclose his name because of security fears.
