To date the Trump administration’s policy for dealing with North Korea’s increasingly aggressive pursuit of nuclear weapons has been to push Beijing to pressure Pyongyang, its longtime ally. It’s an approach that resembles the Obama administration strategy and, observers say, has shown no greater success in slowing Pyongyang’s steady march towards developing nuclear weapons and the missile capability to deliver them as far as North America.   " If ever there was a wake up to call to greater action, it’s Otto’s death," said Bruce Klingner, a senior research fellow at the Heritage Foundation’s Asian Studies Center.
