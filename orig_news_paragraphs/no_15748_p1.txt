The week started with Kathy Griffin and a severed head, and ended with Bill Maher and a word too offensive to print.   For comedians, what defines "too far"    and, more importantly, the optics around it    can land them in hot water or even end their careers. Griffin and Maher are not the first comedians to push the envelope or cross a line. But in the age of social media, when a joke lands flat or goes too far, the outcry can spread like wildfire.
