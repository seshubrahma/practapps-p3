Nevertheless, the problems apparently persisted, and a week before the 1984 primary election, Sessions said he received a call from the district attorney informing him that two black Democratic officials, Reese Billingslea and Warren Kinard, whose candidacies were opposed by Albert Turner, "were very concerned that a concerted effort was being made to deny a fair election" through the use of absentee ballots. Billingslea wrote a letter on Sessions’ behalf for the 1986 confirmation hearing in which he expressed his appreciation for Sessions’ "professionalism" and role in the investigation. " I was one of the first black candidates elected in Perry County Alabama," Billingslea wrote.
