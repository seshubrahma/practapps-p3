There are two ways to look at Rubio’s involvement in the 2013 debate on immigration reform: It was clearly an issue that, because of Rubio’s popularity with both the Tea Party conservatives and Hispanics, could give him a platform, but it was also the kind of   risk that went against Rubio’s basic political character. It required him to take an actual stand. Rubio’s history on immigration had so far been, like much of the rest of him, a study in contradiction.
