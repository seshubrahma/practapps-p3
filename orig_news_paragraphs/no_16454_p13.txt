Democratic leaders, led by New York Sen. Chuck Schumer, lobbied Bayh hard to jump into the race for months. And with Democrats hoping for a wave election spawned by Donald Trump’s unpopularity, Bayh ultimately decided to pull the trigger last month, prompting the party’s candidate, Baron Hill, to drop out of the race and clear the way for the former senator. Bayh’s   life faces scrutiny, When he abandoned his bid for a third Senate term in 2010, Bayh bashed the Senate, saying there was not enough "practical  " and too much "narrow ideology." "I do not love Congress," he said.
