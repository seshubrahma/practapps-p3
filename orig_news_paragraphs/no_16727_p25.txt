Bush pollster Matthew Dowd told journalists the Massachusetts senator was "the best debater since Cicero," referring to the Roman philosopher and orator. His comment, a classic of the expectations setting genre, might also have been a    acknowledgment of the absurdity of   spin.   But it hit the mark, framing Kerry as the favorite    even though Bush arrived at the debate with all the trappings of the presidency. Bush didn’t sustain serious political damage during the debate and went on to win the election, partly as a result of his team’s savvy spin operation.
