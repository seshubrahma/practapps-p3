The process, months in the planning but mere hours in its militarylike execution, unfolds mostly away from public view as Americans and the world focus on the pageantry of Inauguration Day: the presidential oath and address at the Capitol, a parade down Pennsylvania Avenue, the   balls. For the roughly 100 people who work in the White House and the employees on hand to help them with perhaps the world’s   moving day, the official events double as useful distractions that keep the exiting president and his family and the incoming occupants   during the work.
