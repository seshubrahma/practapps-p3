While the former South Carolina Governor, who so far has been outspoken in her brief tenure in New York, said she is supposed to remain somewhat guarded as the leader of the   council, she told a packed news conference Monday that should would always be "a transparency gal."   The role won’t enable Haley to snap her fingers and get whatever she wants on the discussion agenda. While Haley is pressing for a human rights discussion April 18, two other permanent members, Russia and China, are not interested, meaning the idea might have to come to a vote to see if the required nine countries support it. Haley’s proposal comes as President Donald Trump and other members of his administration    notably Secretary of State Rex Tillerson    have put less of a focus on human rights in dealings with world leaders when compared to past administrations, including President Barack Obama’s.
