Nixon was driven from office by the Watergate scandal in August 1974, and the Democrats went on to win a huge victory in the November 1974 midterm elections. Whereupon the Democrats, enjoying nearly 2:1 margins in both houses of Congress —  and determined to root out anything that had Nixon’s fingerprints —  sought, out of sheer spite, to undo the peace deal and cut off any more US aid to South Vietnam. And so they did.
