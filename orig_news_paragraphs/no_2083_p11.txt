She has said she would hold a popular referendum, à la Britain, on French membership in the European Union   —   a test of public will that mainstream French politicians, she says, are afraid to have. From the mood here in southern France, a "Frexit" push would probably prevail in a referendum, just as 55 percent voted in 2005 against the Constitution that a former French president, Valéry Giscard d’Estaing, had drafted. The French "non" killed the treaty, intended to create new federal institutions for a bloc enlarged the year before by 10 countries, mostly former   countries like Slovakia, Poland, the Czech Republic and Hungary.
