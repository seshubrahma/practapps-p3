Perhaps the most striking thing about these criticisms is how easy it would be for the CDC to have its cake and eat it. " There are really basic due process steps that could be put in place that would not undermine the CDC’s powers, but that would put in checks and balances so that when the powers are exercised, it’s done in a way that respects civil liberties," says Alexandra Phelan from Georgetown Law School.    The stickier problem is that there’s little evidence to support screening or quarantining travelers.
