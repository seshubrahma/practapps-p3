The money cannot come soon enough to strategists and campaign officials in the most contested states, many of which, feature a competitive presidential race and   campaigns for Senate and governor. The situation is particularly urgent in Florida, the largest of the traditional presidential swing states, which this year is holding a   contest for Senator Marco Rubio’s seat, the outcome of which could determine control of the Senate. The usually   Florida Republican Party has encountered financial difficulties since Gov. Rick Scott’s preferred candidate for state party chairman was rejected last year, making it more reliant on national assistance. What is more, the sort of     efforts that are typically funded by the parties are especially crucial in Florida, because many of its voters cast absentee ballots or take advantage of early voting.
