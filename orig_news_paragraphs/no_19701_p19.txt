The story is not just about New York beating rural Illinois, but New York beating St. Louis. Government policies that had made the country more equal across regions —  among them antitrust protections —  were forgotten or rolled back in the late 20th century. And fortunes diverged. According to a report from the Economic Innovation Group, an   research and advocacy organization, the economy is growing more and more reliant on a smaller and smaller number of " " counties, some of them in or near Los Angeles, Miami, and New York City.
