A century ago, Prohibition was written into the Constitution —  even if it was soon to be written out. More recently, in 1938, one Wilbert Lee "Pappy" O’Daniel, a popular country singer, ran for governor of Texas on a simple platform: The Ten Commandments. O’Daniel won the election, although in light of the corruption that shadowed his political career, it’s safe to say that the Decalogue was not advanced during his time in Austin.
