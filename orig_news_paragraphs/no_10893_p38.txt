Moreover, a strong Christian faith animated many Populists in the rural areas, they were mostly Protestants, and in the urban areas, mostly Catholic.   Yet in both locales, clergymen were often leaders of the movement they were godly fighters for economic justice for their flocks.   In July 1892, the Populists, convening in Omaha, nominated James B. Weaver of Iowa, a former Republican Congressman, as their candidate for president, and James G. Field, the former Democratic Attorney General of Virginia, to be the   nominee.
