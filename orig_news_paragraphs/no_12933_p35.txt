On Nov. 23, 1918, less than two weeks after the end of World War One, the British Prime Minister David Lloyd George set forth a new agenda for his country: "What is our task?   To make Britain a fit country for heroes to live in. "  Those words were quickly streamlined into the phrase, "A nation fit for heroes," and that’s been a national watchword ever since. Veterans, we can observe, confront all the health threats as anyone else, plus, of course, the burden of their war injuries, including PTSD.    Indeed, it’s likely that vets are especially at risk from the "white death.
