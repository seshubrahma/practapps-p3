Conservative and moderate Republicans have raised concerns about key provisions within the bill. Conservatives say have argued that refundable tax credits are little more than a new entitlement program and some Republicans from   states have said they would not support plans that could kick millions of people off the Medicaid rolls. Bowing to pressure from the right, House leaders instituted an income cap on the tax credit to prevent wealthier Americans from claiming it.   The House plan would also retain the   Cadillac tax    which has never gone into affect    in order to hit the budget targets required under the maneuver used to pass the bill, called budget reconciliation.
