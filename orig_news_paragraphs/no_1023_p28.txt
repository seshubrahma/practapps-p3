It is part of legacy that continues to give a good many French people pause. Some 58 percent of them still consider the Front a "danger" for democracy, according to a poll published in Le Monde this month, 11 percentage points more than in 2013. Yet there is enough uncertainty over whether Ms. Pen has sufficiently softened up traditional resistance that, for the first time, the candidate of a party still considered extremist by many has become the person to beat.
