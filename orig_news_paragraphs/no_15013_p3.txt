However, even if both were to side with Republicans, it wouldn’t be enough to avoid the filibuster.   The majority party can still get around the filibuster by changing Senate rules and using the controversial "nuclear option" to lower the threshold needed to end debate. Democrats, in the face of strong Republican opposition, did the same in 2013 to confirm lower court nominees.   Now Republicans, citing 2013 as a precedent, feel that it’s their only choice.
