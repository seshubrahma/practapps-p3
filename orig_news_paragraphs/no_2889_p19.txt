The final break between Mr. Kerry and Mr. Netanyahu  —   and their patrons, Mr. Obama and Mr. Trump   —   came after years of conversations that never led to the accord the secretary sought. Mr. Kerry made a point of talking with Mr. Netanyahu often, more than was publicly known, hoping to build a relationship that would enable them to bridge their ideological differences. But Mr. Netanyahu never viewed it as in his interest, or in Israel’s, to go along with the American interloper.
