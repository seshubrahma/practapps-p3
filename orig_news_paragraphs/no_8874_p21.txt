Glass invented people, organizations, and events that lived down to his publishers’ darkest expectations of every social group and profession except their own. He was so productive, and so good at fabricating "evidence" to back up his claims, that it simply didn’t occur to his marks that he might be faking so much of his work. (A fascinating 1998 Vanity Fair account of Glass’ downfall noted that he instantly whipped up a phony website for the software company he invented for his final phony article, and drafted his brother to leave phone voice mails in the role of an imaginary company executive, when he learned   were digging into the story.)
