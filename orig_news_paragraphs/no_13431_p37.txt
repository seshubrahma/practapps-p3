In 1960, for instance, Richard Nixon, the incumbent vice president, was defeated by John F. Kennedy.   Nixon’s defeat —  even in a close election that might, in fact, have been stolen —   was a discrediting not only of Nixon, but also of the entire   Republican establishment.   And so party activists, instinctively and ideologically   gathered around the iconic Sen. Barry Goldwater as they looked ahead to the next election in 1964.
