That is, industrialization could turn docile or fatalistic peasants, spread out thinly across the countryside, into angry and   proletarians, concentrated in the big cities —   and that was a formula for unrest, even revolution. Indeed, it was not until the 20th century that every country —  including China, a great civilization, long asleep under decadent imperial misrule —  figured out that it had no choice other than to industrialize.
