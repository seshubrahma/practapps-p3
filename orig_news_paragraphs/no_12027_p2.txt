The mostly minimum wage strikers demanded a "living wage," better working conditions, and the right to form a union without retaliation to address what they called wage theft. The series of U. S. strikes grew to reach 200 cities by April 2015. Despite 30 states and Washington, D. C. already paying higher minimum wages than what is required under federal law, both California Gov. Jerry Brown and New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo signed bills in April 2016 to raise the minimum wage in steps to $15 dollars an hour for the first time in any state.
