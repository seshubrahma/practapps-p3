The order also calls on agencies to offset the cost of any new regulation by eliminating old regulations. Exactly how this would be done is not spelled out. Other countries, including the United Kingdom and Canada, have had some success with such "regulatory offset programs," said Susan Dudley, the director of George Washington University’s Regulatory Studies Center. Such policies, Dudley explained, have "potential not only to reduce the accumulation of regulatory burdens, but to force evaluation of what has and hasn’t worked in the past.
