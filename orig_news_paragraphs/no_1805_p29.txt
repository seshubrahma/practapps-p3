Was she too strong, physically and intellectually, to be a proper first lady? After a decade under a public microscope, she has managed what no other first lady  —   and few people in any public position   —   have succeeded in doing: She has lived a public life without sacrificing her privacy and authenticity. She made her husband both more human and effective as a president by being his interpreter and defender, but also someone we knew was capable of being his critic.
