For the California voters who soon made him governor, the ads created a sense of Reagan as a certain kind of character: the kindly paterfamilias, a trustworthy and nonthreatening guardian of the white   suburban enclave. Years later, the producers of "The Apprentice" carefully crafted a Trump character who was the quintessence of steely resolve and   mastery. American voters noticed. Linda Lucchese, a Trump convention delegate from Illinois who had never previously been involved in politics, told me that she watched "The Apprentice" and decided that Trump would make a perfect president.
