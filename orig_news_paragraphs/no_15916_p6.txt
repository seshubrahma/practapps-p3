Ossoff, meanwhile, has sought to run a centrist campaign    studiously avoiding even saying Trump’s name in an effort to make himself an acceptable alternative for typically conservative voters who might be inclined to buck the GOP.   The 36, 000 early voters who didn’t participate in the primary at all are among the major question marks that make the early vote data hard to read for partisan advantages.   In analyzing that group, Tom Bonier, the CEO of the Democratic voter targeting firm TargetSmart, pointed out that they are less likely to be white than all other segments of voters    and 25% of them are under the age of 35. That demographic analysis figures to benefit Ossoff.
