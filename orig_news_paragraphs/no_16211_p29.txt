However, as a historically popular program, it is unlikely that Medicare will be allowed to go bankrupt.   Despite being criticized by the likes of former President Ronald Reagan, the program has continued to expand since its creation    under both Democrats and Republicans. In fact, even several   conservatives have expanded it    including Reagan, former President Richard Nixon and George W. Bush.   It seems likely that the next president, whoever it may be, would look for ways to shore up Medicare, rather than let it die.
