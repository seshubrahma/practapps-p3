One potential argument for defense lawyers is a principle called neutral reportage, which defends the publishing of some defamatory material if it is a matter of public interest and "does it in a fair and disinterested manner, without endorsing a defamatory charge," Mr. Abrams said. The principle has been rejected by some courts, however, and has not been tested widely, he added.
