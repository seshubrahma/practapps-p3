Austria, Austria looked at electing the EU’s first   head of state since the Second World War when it picked its next president on the same day as Italy’s critical constitutional reforms ballot. Norbert Hofer of the Freedom Party of Austria (FPO) had ridden a populist wave to challenge for presidential power (albeit in a largely ceremonial role). The race had appeared close, but on Sunday Hofer conceded to   independent Alexander Van der Bellen when early returns ran against him. The     candidate campaigned hard on the issue of migrants, calling for a complete end to immigration in a bid to preserve the country’s "ethnic culture."
