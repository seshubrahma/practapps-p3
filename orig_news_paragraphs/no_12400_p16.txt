We liked their smiles and battles, but we never could say their names. Yet through it all, the English yeomanry kept its stolid cool. Skipping ahead centuries, Chesterton lyricized the brave English in their battles against Napoleon the courageous redcoats went overseas to fight, even as the families of many of them were still little more than serfs. Yet although the poem was composed more than a century ago, some of its lines read as if they were written by a contemporary.
