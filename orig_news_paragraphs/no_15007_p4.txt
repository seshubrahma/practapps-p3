Also on the table, Meadows said, was the idea that insurers could opt out of community health ratings, which currently ban insurers from charging higher premiums based on gender or health history. Meadows continued to stress that while he was pleased with the progress, there wasn’t any final deal. Members of the House Freedom Caucus have been the target of the White House’s ire in recent days, with Trump tweeting directly at specific members after they were opposed to the health care bill. When asked about timeline, Meadows demurred saying only that he didn’t want to put an "artificial" deadline on the process.
