It included an agreement to cut trade tariffs on manufactured and agricultural goods, harmonize approaches to intellectual property and to establish an agreed arbitration process by which investors can sue member governments if they violate the terms of the agreement.   It is a mistake, however, to view the TPP only as an economic agreement.   It is best understood as an economic dimension to President Barack Obama’s decision during his first term to rebalance America’s strategic resources to the Pacific to face a rising China.   This rebalance included the redeployment of military assets, the investment of increased diplomatic resources and    through the TPP    a renewed US economic interest in the region.
