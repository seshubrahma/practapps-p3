The study, titled, "Social Media and Fake News in the 2016 Election," was   by economists Matthew Gentzkow of Stanford University and Hunt Allcott of New York University.   The paper utilized web browsing data, a database of   fake news stories, and a   online survey about news trends. Warren reported on the survey results: In sum, they conclude that the role of social media was overstated, with television remaining by far the primary vehicle for consuming political news. Just 14 percent of Americans deemed social media the primary source of their campaign news, according to their research.
