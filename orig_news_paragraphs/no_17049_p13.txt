In our new century, the time has come to replace the war scenario with community policing as a   approach that supports   partnerships and pragmatic   in which police and citizens work together to address public safety challenges ranging from social disorder, to street crime, to fear of crime. When it was conceived in the 20th century, community policing was focused narrowly on the neighborhood and even the beat. Years ago, when I was an officer with the   Police Department, we even focused community policing efforts on one single housing project, which was notorious for a level of violence that intimidated and endangered residents of the project.   The scope of   community policing expands from the intensely local to the broadly global.
