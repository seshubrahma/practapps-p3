The debate inside Mr. Trump’s wide circle of formal and informal advisers  —   pitting supporters of one leading contender, Mitt Romney, against those of another, Rudolph W. Giuliani   —   has led to the kind of dramatic airing of differences that characterized Mr. Trump’s unconventional and often squabbling campaign team. And it traces the outlines of the enduring split in the Republican Party between establishment figures who scoffed at Mr. Trump’s chances of victory and the   insurgents who backed him as a disrupter of the Washington power structure.
