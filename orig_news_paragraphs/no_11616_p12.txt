Despite being in his seventies —  only a few years older than the new President —   Sanders is revered by many as the fresh face of a resurgent American brand of socialism, which was firmly   braced by the new commander in chief in her drive to win at all costs. Officially, Sanders’s role in the new administration will be fixing "income inequality" —  by any means necessary. However, the   friend to socialists around the world —   who took his wife to the former Soviet Union for their honeymoon —  is expected to advise Clinton on foreign policy matters as well, especially on how the United States can integrate more fully into worldwide collectivist movements.
