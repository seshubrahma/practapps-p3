His most   turn, a debate with Vice   Mike Pence, attracted a smattering of critics, who chafed at his repeated interruptions of his opponent onstage. Mostly though, Mr. Kaine cultivated an unpretentious image during the campaign, introducing   comedy viewers to his assemblage of harmonicas, which remain in his briefcase, and urging voters to choose a "you’re hired" president instead of a "you’re fired" president. He often took the stage with a flurry of     gesticulations, like a   coach flashing signals, proving proficient at vigorous fist pumps and seemingly indiscriminate  . He once described life as a   nominee, playfully, as being "kidnapped. "
