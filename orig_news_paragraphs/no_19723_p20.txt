The handgun —  which had been a luxury item in the days of the   flint pistol —  was now a viable weapon for the ordinary citizen interested in   and killing small game. But lower prices alone did not cause gun ownership to spread. The California Gold Rush and the opening of the Far West to settlement increased population and activity in the contested borderlands between the United States and Mexico and around Native American tribes. The expansion of slavery into unsettled districts and the growth of large plantations (coupled with abolitionist agitation) led to more arming of planters’ households and patrols.
