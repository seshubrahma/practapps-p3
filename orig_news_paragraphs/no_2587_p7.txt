For evangelicals, "accepting Christ" is at the heart of becoming a genuine Christian, and refers to acknowledging sin and declaring the need for Jesus Christ as savior. " The expectation evangelicals have is of a radical change, a   turn from the life of sin to following Christ," said Kedron Bardwell, a political science professor at Simpson College in Iowa, who is the son of an evangelical pastor. With new believers, this is often done in prayer with another Christian, which may have been what Dr. Dobson was referring to when he said that he knew the person who had "led him to Christ.
