—   La La Land, the consummate film about filmmaking, emerged with the most Globe awards ever granted in one evening —  but also about its sense of its own moral purpose. The telecast presented itself, for all its wacky antics, as an epic battle: kindness versus cruelty, good versus its absence, a Hollywood that emphasizes inclusion and understanding —   "we have to remind each other of the privilege and the responsibility of the act of empathy," Streep put it —  pitted against a Washington that so often fails to see beyond the self.
