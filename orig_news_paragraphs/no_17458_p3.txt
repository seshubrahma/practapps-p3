Iowa’s election angst, Charlotte’s night of chaos , Zach Locke remembers it vividly: the pungent taste of the tear gas, the ringing sound of gunshots and the chaos and confusion of a city erupting in anger. It was September 21 and Locke, along with his roommate, joined hundreds of others who had descended on Charlotte to protest the fatal police shooting of Keith Lamont Scott.   Before Scott, there had been Alton Sterling and Philando Castile.
