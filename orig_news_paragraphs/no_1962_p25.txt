The   festival starts Saturday with music, dancing, a parade and thousands of tamales. Interest in secession got a boost in California this month after the victory of Donald J. Trump. But another sort of rebellion occurred in Northern California long before Election Day. It was this week in 1941 that a group of miners halted traffic along Route 99 in Siskiyou County and handed out proclamations to motorists declaring the formation of Jefferson, a new state named after the author of the Declaration of Independence.
