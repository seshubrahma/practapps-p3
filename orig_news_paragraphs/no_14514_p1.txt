Trump interviewed several candidates to replace Flynn on Sunday, according to White House spokeswoman Sarah Sanders. " He did several interviews and   meetings that are still ongoing as of this afternoon talking to different candidates for the national security adviser position," she told reporters. On Saturday, White House spokesman Sean Spicer told reporters that Trump planned to speak to former US ambassador to the United Nations John Bolton, acting national security adviser Gen. Keith Kellogg, West Point Superintendent Lt. Gen. Robert Caslen, Lt. Gen. H. R. McMaster, who directs the Army Capabilities Integration Center, and "potentially more" candidates.
