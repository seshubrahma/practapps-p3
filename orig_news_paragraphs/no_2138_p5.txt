Since the election, Sprint shares have risen nearly 40 percent, partly on hopes that it could be acquired by its rival  . While antitrust officials under President Obama have been wary of deals in the telecom sector, Mr. Son and investors are hoping the Trump administration might look more favorably on any potential deal. In January, as part of a restructuring effort, Sprint cut 2, 500 jobs in call centers across the United States and at its headquarters in Overland Park, Kan. On Wednesday, the company said in a statement that it would be creating or bringing back jobs in a variety of departments, including customer care and sales, and that all would be added by the end of its 2017 fiscal year.
