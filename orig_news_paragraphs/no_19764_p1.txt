Last night’s Golden Globe Awards placed a spotlight on the films La La Land and Moonlight —   and on Meryl Streep, who used her lifetime achievement speech to make a political statement against the bullying behavior of   Donald Trump. She also rose to the defense of Hollywood, whose "outsiders and foreigners," she implied, are in a position to model empathy for all of America. But the full picture is more complicated: Mixed martial arts, which Streep invoked as a symbol of provincialism, is in fact a highly multicultural sport.
