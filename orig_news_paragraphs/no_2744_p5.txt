Each event under consideration must be vetted by the overlapping agencies responsible for securing the transfer of power, including the Secret Service, responsible for a president’s security, and the National Park Service, which controls the Mall in Washington. " You have five gigantic security groups that dictate what can and can’t be done," Mr. Barrack said. For example, Mr. Barrack said that hopes to open the White House to visitors, as Andrew Jackson did during his 1829 "People’s Inauguration" (and as other presidents did afterward) had simply proven unrealistic.
