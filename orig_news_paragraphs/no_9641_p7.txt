Hence the Four Freedoms, as a statement of timeless American values, stood out as a bright beacon in a darkened world. FDR’s   vision —   political liberty, religious tolerance, social compassion, and national security —  was an immediate success, resonating deeply with the American public. In 1943, in the pages The Saturday Evening Post, the brilliant illustrator Norman Rockwell immortalized the Four Freedoms in a quartet of memorable paintings.
