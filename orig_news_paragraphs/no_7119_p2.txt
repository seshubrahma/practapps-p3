Exit polling showed the economy’s future was a major   topic for presidential voters across the nation. In California, where presidential votes went heavily in favor of Trump’s opponent, Hillary Clinton, confidence fell for two months in advance of the election, pushing the state’s optimism to a   low in October. The index then rose in November and December in the largest   surge in nearly two years. The Conference Board also reported a surge in consumer confidence nationwide: The Conference Board Consumer Confidence Index® which had increased considerably in November, posted another gain in December.
