David Weil, a professor of management at the Questrom School of Business at Boston University who headed the Labor Department’s wage and hour division at the end of the Obama administration, calls this process the "fissuring" of the workplace. He traces it to the 1980s, when corporations under pressure to raise quarterly profits started shedding "noncore" tasks. The trend grew as the spread of information technology made it easier for companies to standardize and monitor the quality of outsourced work.
