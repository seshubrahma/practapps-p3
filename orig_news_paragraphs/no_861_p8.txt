In Germany, the   Alternative for Germany sold gold bars and coins in a strategy to inflate its revenue and, through a quirk of the rules, increase its access to public funds, until the practice was banned by Parliament. German parties have also sought to divert public funds provided to parliamentary caucuses. " It’s illegal but basically done everywhere" in Germany, said Christoph Möllers, a professor of public law and legal philosophy at Humboldt University of Berlin.
