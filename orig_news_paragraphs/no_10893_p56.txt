Indeed, on most issues, fully reflecting his Alabama heritage, he was, in fact, an unapologetic New Dealer that is, he ran on the traditional   issue of good jobs at good wages.   For example, Wallace extolled the dams built by the New   Tennessee Valley Authority.   The TVA was revered in the South because it had brought relief from floods and   disease in addition, the inexpensive hydropower that the dams generated had attracted industry and jobs.
