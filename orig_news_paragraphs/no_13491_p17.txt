And we know this, too, as context: At the time, in the early 70s, China and the Soviet Union were veering apart. Sharing a   border, heaving with unresolved boundary disputes, the two communist countries, in 1969, had even collided in a brief armed conflict. In other words, in diplomatic terms, Nixon had plenty of leverage on both states. That is, he could bargain to make the US the superior "two out of three" —  that is, siding with one against the other —  in the   game of   geopolitics.
