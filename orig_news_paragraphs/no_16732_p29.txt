Hillary is neither the demon of the right’s perception, nor a feminist saint, nor is she particularly emblematic of her time   —   perhaps more   than modern. Hers is a story of strength and vulnerability, a woman’s story. She is an intelligent woman endowed with energy, enthusiasm, humor, tempestuousness, inner strength, spontaneity in private, lethal (almost) powers of retribution,   lines that come from deep wounds, and the language skills of a sailor (and of a minister) all evidence of her passion  —   which, down deep, is perhaps her most enduring and even endearing trait. ...
