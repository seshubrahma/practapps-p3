For the Chinese government, as for many other governments around the world, Mr. Trump’s freewheeling diplomacy poses a challenge. At first, China’s foreign minister, Wang Yi, played down the episode, saying it was a "petty action by the Taiwan side" that would not upset the longstanding policy of One China. But hours later, the Chinese Foreign Ministry lodged a formal complaint with the Obama administration. It urged the United States to "handle issues related to Taiwan carefully and properly to avoid causing unnecessary interference to the overall U. S.  relationship. "
