Japan’s legislature does not fare much better on this score. Its apportionment of representatives was never fully updated to reflect   migration to urban areas after World War II, when the country rebuilt its   cities and quickly industrialized. In 2013, the country’s highest court ruled that Japan’s elections existed "in a state of unconstitutionality," due to the extra voting power wielded by rural voters.
