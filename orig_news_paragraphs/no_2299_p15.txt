A Times review of WikiLeaks’ activities during the four years its founder, Julian Assange, has been holed up in the Ecuadorean Embassy in London shows that the group’s document releases have often benefited Russia, frequently at the expense of the West. In an interview, Mr. Assange called Russia a "bit player on the world stage" and said that scrutinizing superpowers like the United States was more relevant. _
