Each was captured by multiple cameras, rolling at around 2, 400 frames per second. A handful of the estimated 10, 000 mesmerizing test films have been declassified and the National Laboratory, custodians of the material, has uploaded more than 60 videos to a YouTube playlist.    The project to digitize the aging film came just in time. Greg Spriggs, a nuclear weapons physicist at the laboratory who’s in charge of the project, says the films were decomposing "to the point where they’ll become useless."
