Even beyond appearances, it’s possible to see why both of them admire Mr. Bowie, that relentless pop chameleon. Mr. Cyrus, though never quite respected for his music or hairstyle, has managed to stretch his   charisma, distinctive look and willingness to engage with the kitschy and the lowbrow into a constantly regenerating career of nearly 25 years. By embracing his cowboy camp factor, and now his aging   essence, Mr. Cyrus, 54, has ambled down a surreal path from his first, biggest hit, "Achy Breaky Heart," in 1992, to a varied acting résumé, including roles in David Lynch ’s "Mulholland Drive" and Disney’s "Hannah Montana," which made Miley a star.
