This is why populists on the left and the right are strong in the countryside. In the Czech Republic, Milos Zeman, the populist   president, draws greater support outside the capital. In Hungary, the   ruling party of Prime Minister Viktor Orban also does best outside the capital of Budapest. In the Baltic nation of Lithuania,   fervor has coalesced around the Peasant’s and Greens Union, which stormed to victory in the fall parliamentary elections on a populist platform and strong rural support leery of the immigrants filing into the country’s cities.
