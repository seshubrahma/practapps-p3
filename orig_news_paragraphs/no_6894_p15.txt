It is, of course, possible to imagine that Republicans could have come up with a true    bill, one that peeled away the mistakes and excesses of Obamacare while maintaining the essential of affordable care for all citizens.   But the latest GOP plan, AHCA, was not it.    Indeed, the reality of the disconnect between Republican theory and political practicality became searingly obvious on March 13, when the Congressional Budget Office concluded that under AHCA, 24 million people would lose their health insurance.   At that moment, the bill was doomed according to a Quinnipiac Poll taken ten days after the CBO finding, just 17 percent of Americans supported AHCA.
