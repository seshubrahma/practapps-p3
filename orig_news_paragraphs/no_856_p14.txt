There was a kind of "time warp" there, she says: "The Civil War was yesterday but 1960 is spoken of as if it were about 300 years ago. " The people Didion interviewed or tried to interview tended to greet her questions with defensive remarks about the pace of change in the South, or with nostalgic and what can only be called racist talk about old ways of life. Although it was 1970, the attitudes Didion encountered can sometimes sound like those described by Harper Lee in "To Kill a Mockingbird," set in 1930s Alabama.
