But a political pardon risks tainting Obama’s presidential legacy, something that Bush also avoided. When Dick Cheney demanded a pardon for his aide Scooter Libby, Bush refused, but commuted his sentence, keeping Cheney’s aide out of jail. Libby still had to pay a $250, 000 fine and serve probation for two years. Cheney vigorously pursued the case, accusing Bush of leaving a wounded soldier on the battlefield.
