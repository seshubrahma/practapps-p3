Sure, Congressional Democrats are voting and speaking out against Trump’s proposals on issues like health care, but few of them can garner national headlines or get a video to go viral.   Comedians, on the other hand, are now the ones with a "bully pulpit" to raise issues in ways that dominate our social media feeds and impact the larger political conversation. We saw an example this week with Jimmy Kimmel’s emotional plea to preserve coverage for   conditions in the   health care bill.   Kimmel touched a nerve, as the video of his tearful plea was widely shared.
