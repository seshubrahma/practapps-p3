Muncie’s jobless rate is 5. 7 percent, less than one percentage point higher than the national rate of 4. 9 percent or Indiana’s 4. 6 percent. But the impact of deindustrialization —  including the exodus of over 10, 000 factory jobs since 1970 in a city of only 70, 000 people —  is visible almost everywhere. Most of the departing companies tore down their factories, leaving lots strewn with weeds and rubble. BorgWarner left a handful of structures —  including a 1. 2    sprawl of red brick and steel near the edge of town.
