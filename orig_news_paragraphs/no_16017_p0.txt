Indianapolis, Indiana (CNN) Hillary Clinton, responding for the first time to the United Kingdom voting to leave the European Union, argued that the vote shows now is a time for "steady, experienced leadership," not "bombastic comments in turbulent times. " While Clinton did not directly mention presumptive Republican candidate Donald Trump, her argument was clear: Trump is not the kind of leader that could guide the United States through all the complications and economic uncertainty that comes with this kind of international incident.
