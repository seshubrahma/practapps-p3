Despite Lavanga ’s portrayal of the two leaders as polar opposites, in point of fact, the Pope and the President have a fair amount of common ground to build on —  arguably more than if Hillary Clinton had been elected. Both leaders oppose abortion and euthanasia, defend religious liberty and conscientious objection, emphasize job creation, support the traditional family, and have spoken out strongly in defense of persecuted Christians in the Middle East and elsewhere. Even on the question of immigration, Pope Francis asserted in January that "every country has the right to control its borders," especially where the risk of terrorism exists.
