"Obviously there is a problem with violence," Norman told CNN, "but I also got the feeling there was a climate of paranoia, feeding in and exaggerating the real problem."   The threat is often existential and "othering" of the black population is still prevalent, a point made clear by the Suidlanders Norman meets, a group of Afrikaner "preppers" not affiliated with Orania, planning for the event of a revolution. Established in 2006, while they "believe the revolution will start with    violence, they fear that aggression will quickly focus on white people," writes the author.
