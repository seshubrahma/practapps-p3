Speaker Paul Ryan and Trump’s nominee for health secretary, Tom Price, have called for turning it into a block grant for the states. But that reform is likely to be far more politically controversial than repealing the Affordable Care Act. States would not immediately lose Medicaid funding under the GOP’s "repeal and delay" strategy, which calls for Obamacare to stay on the books for as long as four years while Republicans try to pass and implement a replacement.
