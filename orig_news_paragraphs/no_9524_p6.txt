History teaches that frustrated expectations and rising inequality —  falling average family incomes alongside   Wall Street traders and software developers earning vast sums —  is a highly combustible combination. The man on the white horse usually provides the match —  often in the form of nationalism and scapegoats. Enter Donald Trump with tough talk —  and little substance —  about leveling the playing field with China, closing the border with Mexico, banning Muslim immigration and rude remarks about successful women.
