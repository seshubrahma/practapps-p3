Set in the course of one night, it eavesdrops on several people who worked together on "Midnight in a Clearing With Moon and Stars," a fictitious flop from a decade ago. They have gathered for a reunion at a rundown club. As they snack and sip and reminisce, they reveal the brutality of the world outside and the ways that artists can abet it, resist it and ignore it. The London production stoked controversy, with The Independent approvingly describing a "disturbing, balefully hilarious new play," while many other critics attacked its tone, pace and politics.
