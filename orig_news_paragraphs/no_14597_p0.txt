McMaster is already setting a strikingly different tone than his ousted predecessor, Michael Flynn, and President Donald Trump, saying the term "radical Islamic terrorism" isn’t helpful for US goals. McMaster said jihadist terrorists aren’t true to their professed religion and that the use of the phrase "radical Islamic terrorism" doesn’t help the US in working with allies to defeat terrorist groups, an official present at the session confirmed to CNN.
