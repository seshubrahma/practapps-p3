By now we should all be familiar with the way unemployment statistics can be sliced and diced for political purposes, so it’s necessary to also consider the kind of   unemployment that falls completely off the media radar screen.    Even those most eager to hype the   "Obama recovery" have been forced to admit   haven’t benefited nearly as much as other demographics.   The black workforce is far smaller relative to the overall population than the white workforce is, while   on both Right and Left consistently find that average black wages are far lower.    One study found that over the past 15 years, the average hourly wage of black workers fell by 44 cents, while white and Hispanic wages rose by 48 and 45 cents during the same period.
