[For the first time in American history, in 2003 Sen. Chuck Schumer ( ) persuaded Senate Democrats to use the upper chamber’s   rule on legislation to block a final vote on judicial nominations, at that time blocking several of George W. Bush’s nominees to the federal appeals courts.   Republicans then considered the constitutional option —   also called the "nuclear option " —  to restore the    practice of always allowing votes on judges, by ruling that filibusters apply to legislation, but not to nominations.
