But Mr. Trump’s early intervention on spending issues also highlighted the many challenges facing the Republican majority as it adapts to the priorities of a new administration. Referring to Mr. Trump’s desire to "drain the swamp," Mr. Ryan halted an effort at a   procedural meeting on Wednesday that would have lifted a ban on earmarks  —   the   practice of allowing lawmakers to allocate money to specific projects in their states or districts   —   promising instead to hold a vote on the proposal early next year. It has been several years since lawmakers passed a spending bill in time for the beginning of the fiscal year.
