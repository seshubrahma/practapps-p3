As the general unemployment rate slowly settled back to "normal" in the final months of the Obama presidency, so too did the black unemployment rate fall below its 2008 and 2009 highs.   As of December, the white unemployment rate was 4. 5 percent, while it’s 9. 5 percent for blacks. However, The Atlantic notes that the current gap between black and white is still slightly larger than the gap from 15 years ago.   It’s even worse for those who lack advanced degrees.
