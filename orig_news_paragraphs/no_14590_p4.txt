Not only did I always know I was black, I always knew we were and are beautiful, culturally rich, and creators of everything from the sciences to the arts.    My father constantly reminded me that he named me after Angela Yvonne Davis, a scholar and activist who was well known for her work in tandem with the Black Panther Party.   That felt like a purposeful, beckoning call to engage in strategic resistance and to fight for the oppressed.    Our name means "bringer of truth" or "messenger of God.
