Clinton’’s reluctance to stand in front of reporters and field   questions goes back to her time as First Lady in the 1990s. Back   then, she faced ethical questions surrounding what she believed   were   scandals and resisted prodding from aides to   confront the press directly. When she finally spent 70 minutes at   a press conference in 1994, she explained her aversion: "I’’ve   always believed in a zone of privacy, and I told a friend the   other day that I feel after resisting for a long time I’’ve been   rezoned.
