In Britain, the Labour Party is in tatters with a leader who appeals to activists but has failed to build a   coalition. Across Europe, the old Socialist blocs have fractured into smaller parties, partly because their voting bases have changed but also because rampant inequality and the decline of the middle class have created fertile ground for more extreme parties. " On the left they are trying to stand up for their old core group, industrial workers," said Steve Coulter, who teaches political economy at the London School of Economics.
