A common practice in Washington is for key power players not to register as a lobbyist, but instead work as a consultant or adviser    allowing them to take their experience and contacts to make hundreds of thousands of dollars on K Street. The announcement comes as Trump’s transition team works to remove lobbyists from its ranks after Vice   Mike Pence took over the efforts from New Jersey Gov. Chris Christie.   The transition operation working before Election Day was staffed with   Washington experts and lobbyists from K Street, think tanks and political offices, drawing questions about Trump’s commitment to reforms. On "60 Minutes" Sunday, Trump acknowledged the transition team was stacked with DC insiders.
