Wilders calls Islam a threat to western democracy and vows to close all mosques and ban the Quran, if he wins power. But he has alienated so much of the political mainstream that even if he wins the popular vote he is considered unlikely to be able to form a ruling coalition in a nation where no single party has ever ruled alone. Crucially, Prime Minister Rutte has ruled out working together after the election. Polls show Rutte’s   People’s Party for Freedom and Democracy with the most voter support in the days leading up to Wednesday’s election.
