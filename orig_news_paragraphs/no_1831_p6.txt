Many analysts thought Mr. Trump’s presidential bid was a long shot because he had little support from nonwhite voters, but a Times analysis found that a campaign reliant on white voters could win. The Upshot found that 2012 exit polls did not count millions of white   voters who had gone to the polls on Election Day. It also provided an interactive that allowed readers to simulate the turnout of various demographic groups. Mrs. Clinton was the first woman to win a major party’s nomination for president, 180 years after a nonwhite man was elected to a state Legislature for the first time.
