At first, factories installed whistles or bells that sounded throughout the area to alert its employees that the workday was set to begin.   Some companies even hired knocker uppers to bang on windows and rouse their employees at their homes.    Then, in 1876, an American company received the first patent for a mechanical alarm.   Even though the concept and the crude models dated back to Plato, these were the first   alarm clocks and they paved the way for a huge improvement in personal and business efficiency.
