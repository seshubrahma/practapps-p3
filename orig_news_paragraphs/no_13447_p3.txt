Clinton supporters have consoled themselves that, Iowa and New Hampshire notwithstanding, Hillary was better positioned nationally against Sanders for the nomination. It was also widely assumed that Clinton would fare better against potential GOP rivals, a fact that would help her greatly has Democrats looked for the strongest candidate in November. That assumption is turning on its head now, however. Sanders, at +9,  has the best favorable ratings of all the candidates, with 44 percent of voters have a positive opinion and 35 percent negative.
