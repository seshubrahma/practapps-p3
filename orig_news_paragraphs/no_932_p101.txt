She was also one of the first senators to grasp the importance, and potential, of the Women’s March, which would descend on Washington the day after the Mattis vote. March organizers say they invited some senators to speak, but Gillibrand came to them asking to be involved. In the days after the Women’s March, the rest of the party began to figure out what Gillibrand had already grasped: Maybe the party’s base wanted the Democrats to act like McConnell. A protest against one aspect of Trump quickly became a protest against all aspects of him.
