Tim Kaine, too, was there to introduce himself, but more importantly present a case for the   ticket that it wouldn’t just address issues like contraception and abortion rights, but also the economic interests of average   voters in the heartland. The St.   Kansas   former Catholic missionary didn’t sound like a Washington insider, nor was he trying to talk to them.
