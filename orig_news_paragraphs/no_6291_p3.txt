For eight years the Republican Party mustered only tepid opposition to President Obama. This was a party made feckless by its incomprehension of the nature of the left, by a failure of nerve, and by a lack of the will to win. Then Trump barreled onto the scene with a readiness to "disregard the politically correct restraints on public discourse" and to "relentlessly attack" Democrats who could dish it out but were unaccustomed to having it thrown back at them. As a result, the "passions Trump aroused in the electorate sparked a revolt in the Republican Party" and thrust the political outsider ahead of his 16 rivals in the primaries and then on to election victory against Hillary Clinton.
