California’s agricultural industry is bigger than ever. It produces roughly a third of all fruits and vegetables grown in the United States and has about a third of the nation’s farmworkers. Philip Martin, a specialist on organized farm labor, paints a less stark picture than Mr. Gould of the plight of farmworkers. Farmworker wages "have gone up significantly," Mr. Martin said, and some workers have the benefit of voluntary agreements that growers sometimes enter with their suppliers.
