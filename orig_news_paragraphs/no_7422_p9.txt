Whenever the Attorney General finds that a commercial airline has failed to comply with regulations of the Attorney General relating to requirements of airlines for the detection of fraudulent documents used by passengers traveling to the United States (including the training of personnel in such detection) the Attorney General may suspend the entry of some or all aliens transported to the United States by such airline. So, in other words, the president —  thanks to the U. S. Congress granting him such power in federal law —   already has the ability to temporarily ban any class of aliens from entering the United States   —   for as long as he wants
