Republicans had anticipated the CBO report would show that millions of people would lose coverage under the GOP proposal. Lawmakers and aides alike preemptively pushed back on the findings even before the CBO released its study, saying the loss of coverage would be due to people electing not to get insurance because they would no longer be mandated to do so. But the loss in coverage is only exacerbated by other details in the CBO report. Fourteen million fewer people would enroll in Medicaid by 2026 under the GOP legislation compared to under Obamacare.
