The unions, a source of support for President Trump during last year’s campaign, welcomed his administration ’s announcement as proof that Mr. Trump would swiftly meet his promises to restore "law and order" to the country. But their view contrasted sharply with those of many police chiefs and politicians who have been living under agreements struck with the Justice Department, who vowed to continue making changes to their police departments with or without the department’s imprimatur. They say that the results of consent decrees, which are backed by a court order, and   memorandums of agreement, which are reached out of court, have been mostly positive, if mixed, and in some cases absolutely essential.
