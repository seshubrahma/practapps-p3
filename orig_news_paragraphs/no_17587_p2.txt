Had Mitt Romney been elected president in 2012, undoing the     Affordable Care Act would have been relatively straightforward,     as the Medicaid expansion and the health insurance exchanges     didn’t kick in until 2014. Repealing Obamacare now would have     huge   effects. It would mean taking insurance     coverage away from millions of people —  unless, that is,     Republicans can coalesce around a replacement that will cover     all those people or at least get in the same ballpark.   ’ ’     Republicans, though, are far less unified around what a     replacement ought to look like than they are in their     opposition to Obamacare.
