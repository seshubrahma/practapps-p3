Brazil:   2016, Like Argentina, the socialist Workers’ Party (PT) ruled Brazil for more than a decade: seven years of President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva, and nearly six of Dilma Rousseff. Under da Silva, the socialists established a sprawling corruption scheme that came to be known by the nickname "Operation Car Wash," in which government officials would systematically overcharge for government projects through   oil corporation Petrobras and keep the change.
