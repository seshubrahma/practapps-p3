Sanders, the candidate surging in the polls both nationally and in Iowa, did not attack Clinton. Around 200 supporters at 20 tables backed Sanders, who delivered a stump speech that promised universal health care,   college and a breakup of the big banks. Chafee opened the event, but only spoke for six minutes, less than half the 15 minutes allotted to each candidate. He used his speech to introduce himself to the audience and outline his views on climate, war and raising the minimum wage.
