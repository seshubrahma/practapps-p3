Today’s threats, whether terrorist or merely criminal, are increasingly networked and dispersed it only makes sense that our response to them must take a similar form. It might sound like science fiction, but, in 20 years’ time, we could very well find that LAX has a stronger international intelligence game than many U. S. allies. LAX field agents could be embedded overseas, cultivating informants, sussing out impending threats. It will be an era of infrastructural intelligence, when airfields, bridges, ports, and tunnels have, in effect, their own internal versions of the C. I. A. —  and LAX will have been there first.
