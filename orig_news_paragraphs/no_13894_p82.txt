Obama’s choices were notable for their diversity. He appointed record numbers of women and minorities to the bench, as well as several openly gay jurists. He appointed Sonia Sotomayor (the first Hispanic) and Elena Kagan (the fourth woman) to the Supreme Court, and they have been strong liberal voices there.   But if their liberal colleagues Ruth Bader Ginsburg (age 83) and Stephen Breyer (78) as well as the moderate Anthony Kennedy (80) leave during Trump’s presidency, the Court will take a conservative turn for at least a generation.
