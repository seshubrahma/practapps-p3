While it’s interesting to see gays and lesbians overcome differences to unite politically, or Jones debate tactics with the more militant leaders of the ACT UP movement, there’s a disjointed quality, especially in the early going, in trying to service the various stories. Any one of these personalities might have made for a powerful biography. As constructed, the approach feels as if it touches upon more and ultimately says less. Perhaps inevitably, "When We Rise" also risks becoming preachy in places, as characters deliver speeches in the course of ordinary dialogue.
