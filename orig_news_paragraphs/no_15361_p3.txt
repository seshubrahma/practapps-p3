The Little Sisters are represented by the Becket Fund, a nonprofit firm that focuses on religious liberty, who helped the nuns gain a key exemption from the Affordable Care Act.   That case    and religious freedom writ large    is what brought together the nuns, the President, and a few dozen religious leaders in the Rose Garden on Thursday. The stated purpose was Trump’s signing of an executive order, which he said would prevent the federal government from "bullying and even punishing Americans for following their religious beliefs."  Both liberals and conservative agree, however, that Trump’s order accomplishes less than advertised.   Robert George, a Princeton University professor and leading expert on religious liberty, called the executive order "meaningless" and "a betrayal.
