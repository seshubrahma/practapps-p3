A bunker up ahead, manned by Norwegian soldiers. Shots ring out as the Marines advance, crunching through the snow beneath gray winter skies. It’s all   of course    the maneuvers are part of a training exercise, but one jarringly imbued with the new reality along NATO’s northernmost border with Russia. Some 300 US Marines are due to be based in Norway on a rotational basis from January, for a year, as part of a package of measures intended to reassure one of NATO’s most easterly members.
