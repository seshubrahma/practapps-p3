A series of controversial and problematic cabinet appointments, including a series of missteps with his Attorney General (Janet Reno, his third choice, was not confirmed until March) left him looking incompetent and stifled progress on his legislation.   His   compromise over whether gays and lesbians could serve in the military, "don’t ask, don’t tell" left many mad with him and few particularly pleased with the outcome.   Clinton also stalled on his legislative agenda while moving away from a   tax cut that he had promised in the campaign. His approval ratings fell from 64% in February 1993 to about 37% in May 1993.
