There have been only two cases when the president’s party gained in both houses of Congress since the Civil War: in 1934 and in 2002. Besides the normal historical cycle of backlash against the White House, Democrats this time around are whetting their lips because House Republicans have just voted for a health care bill that rescinds benefits for millions of   Americans.   This could prove to be a policy victory that ends in political defeat. Between the midterms’ track record and the polarizing health care legislation, this week’s events would seem to be a recipe for political disaster for the GOP.
