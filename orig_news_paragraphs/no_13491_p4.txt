The American effort to thwart communism might have been, as Ronald Reagan later said in 1980, a "noble cause," but at the time, it was a deeply unpopular cause. By the November 1968 US presidential elections, after the death of more than 35, 000 Americans in the fighting, the American people had turned against the war. And so a new man, Richard Nixon, was elected that year. His pledge was peace, but also, he declared, it would be "peace with honor.
