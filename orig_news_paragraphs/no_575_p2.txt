Undocumented immigrants are bracing for a surge in deportations as the federal government prepares to follow through on Mr. Trump’s campaign pledge to crack down on illegal immigration. A case in Phoenix illustrated their fears: Guadalupe García de Rayos, 35, a married mother of two who sneaked across the border 21 years ago, was deported to Nogales, Mexico, after a routine   with the federal immigration agency. Her abrupt removal "illustrates the new reality," the Mexican government said, warning its citizens in the U. S. to "take precautions and to keep in touch with their nearest consulate. "
