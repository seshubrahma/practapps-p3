To those who might be inclined to tell artists to shut up and sing or act, the evening offered a concerted rejoinder, subjecting President Trump to rebukes that ranged from mockery to more sober indictments to stirring statements about unity and how recent government actions are antithetical to American values. The same themes emerged the night before, at the untelevised Producers Guild of America honors. That ceremony also heightened the more prosaic sense that "La La Land" is the movie to beat for this year’s Oscar, given a historic correlation between winning there and the Academy Awards. Obviously, not everyone agrees with the politics expressed, and it’s become a popular trope to criticize    stars for lecturing the rest of the country.
