Indeed, the cumulative trade deficit over the last 45 years has totaled more than $12 trillion.    And what has the impact of that trade deficit been on US economic growth?   Well, the standard formula for gross domestic product (GDP) is private consumption spending, plus savings, plus government spending, plus (or minus, as the case may be) the balance of trade.   And so we can see that our cumulative trade deficit has subtracted $12 trillion from the US economy since the 70s.
