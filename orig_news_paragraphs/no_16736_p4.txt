Simply put, greatness in American politics is the exception, not the norm. Tough act to follow, Second, Roosevelt set a bar of achievement so high that it’s impossible to imagine any future president surpassing it. Roosevelt led the country through its most terrifying economic crisis and to victory in its greatest war.   Ironically, when Republicans pushed through the 22nd Amendment to the Constitution back in 1951 to restrict a president to no more than two consecutive terms    in an effort to ensure that there would be no chance of another Roosevelt figure being elected to four terms    they virtually ensured the Democrat’s place in the pantheon of greatness.
