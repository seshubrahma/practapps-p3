A closer look at the disruptions that moving toward a "  cash flow tax" would create makes clear why it would be no small event. The tax has a feature called "border adjustment," under which exports are not taxed but imports are. That, at first glance, may seem to penalize companies that import goods, like retailers, and subsidize those that export, like makers of jumbo jets. But economists believe the change in the tax code would lead to shifts in the currency markets that offset those moves, namely to a sharp rise in the value of the dollar compared with other currencies.
