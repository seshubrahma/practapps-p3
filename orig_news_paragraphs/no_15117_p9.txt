While CNN has not independently confirmed reports of China refusing North Korean coal ships, Huang Songping, a Chinese customs spokesman, said Thursday that China hadn’t imported any coal from North Korea since February 18.   However, the overall trade volume between both countries actually went up 37. 4% in the first quarter of 2017, according to Chinese customs officials. Ashley Townshend, a research fellow at the United States Studies Centre at the University of Sydney, says while the coal import ban is useful, it’s not going to stop North Korea firing off missiles or conducting another nuclear test
