In most recent elections, Republicans have tended to nominate   candidates who were seen as having the best prospects in the general election. Even after the 2010 tea party takeover in the House, the GOP nominated Mitt Romney  —   the former governor of moderate Massachusetts   —   in the 2012 presidential race. Four years later, many Republican voters not only believe that nominating a centrist would cost them another shot at the White House, but they also are deeply skeptical that an establishment GOP president would follow through on their priorities.
