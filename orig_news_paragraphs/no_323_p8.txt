The search giant said it reviewed 550 sites "suspected of misrepresenting content to users, including impersonating news organizations" in November and December. It took action against 340 of those sites and kicked nearly 200 publishers off its network permanently. Google was careful not to say that these were fake news sites, only sites that deceive users by misrepresenting themselves or their content. This month, Media Matters noted that Google changed the language of its advertising policy, removing the words "fake news.
