Indeed, the head of the Socialist Party back then condemned the Populists as "bourgeois. " In fact, most Populists saw themselves as   people who were threatened from both above and below.   That is, they felt squeezed from above by predatory plutocrats who finagled the system at their expense, and squeezed from below by itinerant tramps, who would work for lower wages —  when they weren’t trying to steal.
