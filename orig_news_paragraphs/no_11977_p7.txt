For all of her firebrand conservatism, Palin owes her place on the national stage to the mainstream Republican Party. She was a   Alaska governor when Sen. John McCain  —   seen by some Republicans as an embodiment of the party establishment   —   tapped her as his running mate for the 2008 election. Palin was an awkward fit as No. 2 on the ticket, but she built an enthusiastic following with conservatives.
