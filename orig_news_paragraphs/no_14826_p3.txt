In France, Marine Le Pen    who wants France to drop the euro and has threatened, like Wilders, to hold a   referendum on quitting the European Union    has made her   National Front party a leading contender in April’s presidential ballot. Meanwhile, German Chancellor Angela Merkel, who’s due to meet with Trump this week, faces attacks from the right over her handling of Europe’s refugee crisis. But Wilders’ popularity has slipped in recent weeks, said Quentin Peel, associate fellow at   think tank Chatham House     and he believes that could be down in part to Trump’s arrival in power.
