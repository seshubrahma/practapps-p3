The official said Cabinet members can try to sway an undecided president by speaking publicly    a path Defense Secretary James Mattis has taken in stating his support for NATO and opposition to torture    or they can keep quiet to see which way the wind blows. They can also try to get the President’s ear and confidence by taking a lower profile. But the official warned, "If you’re not clearly drawing your line on an issue, no one is going to respect it." There are costs to silence from the State Department, many current and former agency officials said, high among them the uncertainty it causes overseas.
