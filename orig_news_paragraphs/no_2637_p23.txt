At the same time, Mr. Furman acknowledged that a large proportion of   workers, 25 to 54, particularly men, remained outside the circle of work. This group’s participation rate, now at 88 percent, started slipping decades ago, he said, but the decline in the United States has been much sharper than in advanced Western European economies. " That leads me to the conclusion that it is not caused by technology but is a result of institutional and policy choices we’ve made as a country," Mr. Furman said.
