First, the tax code is much simpler. A US multinational firm engages in three types of transactions: between its foreign affiliates and others abroad, between the domestic company and its foreign affiliates, and between the domestic company and others in the United States. To compute their tax under the House proposal, firms operating in the United States would need only to report transactions of the last type    domestic transactions. This limited focus also makes it easier to verify that firms are complying with the tax code.
