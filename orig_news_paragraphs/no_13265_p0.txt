President Barack Obama set the   record in November 2016 for the number of regulatory pages in the Federal Register at 81, 640 pages, but in the last month he has pushed that record up by over 12 percent to 91, 642 pages. [The Federal Register as the official journal of the federal government of the United States and contains government agency rules, proposed rules, and public notices.   When Barack Obama was sworn in as the 44th President of the United States in January 2009, the Federal Register stood at a record 79, 435 pages, according to the Foundation for Economic Education. The Federal Register grew slowly to 80, 250 pages over the next seven years, and then a little faster to 81, 640 pages in early November 2016.
