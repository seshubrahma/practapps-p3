Climate change can impact human infectious disease via the pathogen itself, via the host or by creating conditions for transmission, according to a study published in the journal Environment International last year. The study involved reviewing research on climate change and health published between 1990 and 2015. For instance, warmer and wetter weather can provide ideal conditions for   mosquitoes to flourish, promoting the spread of   diseases.
