Infrastructure investment in struggling parts of the country —  building rural prisons, say, or constructing a beautiful wall through a sparsely populated desert —  would help, though such implementation can take time. Policies to help certain industries through tariffs or tax incentives or government stimulus —  punishing foreign automakers, perhaps, or subsidizing American steel companies —  might even things out as well, and Trump has shown a remarkable willingness to meddle in companies’ internal affairs and boost certain businesses over others. But the truth is that the forces holding rural America back and pushing urban America forward are profound ones.
