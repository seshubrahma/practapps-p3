It’s conservative, moderate, liberal as objective as possible but sometimes not struggling to survive in the age of the Internet.   Hard to define, in other words    and yet in recent years it has developed a sense of itself, as if it had some unifying political purpose. Choosing Bob Woodward and Carl Bernstein, the men who exposed the Watergate scandal that toppled Richard Nixon, to address the correspondents’ dinner sent a clear message: the press is not only here to hold presidents to account but to bring them down. Were that goal pursued without bias, it would be less controversial.
