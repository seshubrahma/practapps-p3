Most educated Americans vaguely remember that the amendment granting women the right to vote was passed by Congress in 1919 and ratified by the states in 1920," the American Spectator’s David Catron wrote back in 2012.   But the number of people who know anything about the   legislative war that preceded that victory is smaller than the audience of MSNBC. That war began in 1878, when a California Republican named A. A. Sargent introduced the 19th Amendment only to see it voted down by a   Congress. It finally ended four decades later, when the Republicans won landslide victories in the House and the Senate, giving them the power to pass the amendment despite continued opposition from most elected Democrats  —   including President Woodrow Wilson, to whom the suffragettes frequently referred as "Kaiser Wilson.
