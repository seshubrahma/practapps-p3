For instance, one country might develop weapons that could deliver warheads more rapidly, which would require the other to shorten its response time and build redundant, retaliatory weapons. While "arms race" describes the sets of policies that helped make the Cold War so dangerous, arms racing was not in itself policy. Rather, it was a    —   and    —   byproduct of American and Soviet aims. Leaders on both sides wanted to avoid losing, but none saw the race as desirable.
