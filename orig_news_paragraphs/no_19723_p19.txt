On this day in 1847, Samuel Colt —  described in our November 1880 issue as "a man of fine presence   …     and of indomitable perseverance" —  sold a thousand of his first revolvers to the Texas Rangers. In our November 2000 issue, Richard Slotkin described how firearms subsequently spread: The techniques of mass production and serial fire perfected by Samuel Colt made guns cheaper to own and easier to use. The handgun —  which had been a luxury item in the days of the   flint pistol —  was now a viable weapon for the ordinary citizen interested in   and killing small game.
