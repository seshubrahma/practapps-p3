By contrast, the Michael Slager trial, being held in the same city at the same time as Roof’s, seemed the more important one. Slager is the North Charleston police officer seen shooting a fleeing man in the back multiple times. Slager was equipped with public dollars, armed and trained with the public’s blessing, given the authority to detain, arrest and even kill with the public’s authority. When such a man violates the trust placed in him, it can undermine a democracy like little else.
