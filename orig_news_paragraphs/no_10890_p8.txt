Theoretical Libertarianism is what it sounds like —  libertarian ideology, not connected to grubby politics.   And Applied Libertarianism is what it sounds like —  libertarian ideology that is connected to grubby politics. We can quickly see that Theoretical Libertarianism appeals to those who seek purity, while Applied Libertarianism appeals to those who seek practicality. And as we shall see, the theoretical and the applied can inhabit, at different times, the same person.
