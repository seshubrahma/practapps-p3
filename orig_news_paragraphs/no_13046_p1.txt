"As vote nears, a more radical Trump emerges," and the report is highly perceptive, except for one crucial detail: The new version of Trump is less radical, not more. One of the important underlying facts of American politics is that rich people tend to have more socially liberal and economically conservative beliefs than the country as a whole. The elite criticism of the structure of party politics usually boils down to demanding that the parties reflect elite beliefs even more closely than they already do. Hence the endless demands for a socially liberal third party that will reduce spending on retirement programs, or the fantasy that America is entering a "libertarian moment.
