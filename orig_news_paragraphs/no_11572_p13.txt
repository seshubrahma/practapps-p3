The same trend is visible in faded industrial pockets from New Jersey and upstate New York to Alabama and Kentucky. According to the data, in about 1, 800 counties across the United States —  nearly 60 percent of those where data is available —  the share of white men in the lowest group of earners has grown over this period. The trend is particularly pronounced in the Rust Belt states of Michigan, Ohio and Indiana.
