The 401( k) began as a technical adjustment to   the tax code, one meant to mostly impact   executives   using   plans. People like Benna, a benefits   consultant, convinced the Reagan administration that the language   of the statute allowed for all employees to put aside a portion   of their salaries on a   basis. It was supposed to   supplement corporate pensions. Instead, in something almost no   one foresaw, the 401( k) replaced them.
