If you’re unfortunate enough to have followed   outrage over video games over the past few years, you are likely to have encountered the "Beat Up Anita Sarkeesian" game. The amateur video game allows players to punch a picture of feminist provocateur Anita Sarkeesian until she’s blue in the face. It was held up as a prime example of society’s   misogyny, with thousands of column inches spent condemning the game and the people who made it. Feminist activists plotted a vast, online public shaming campaign to ruin the life of the young game developer who created it.
