New polling data from Reuters shows a surge in support for a   temporary ban on any Muslim entry to the United States. [In the wake of the terrorist attack in Orlando, 50 percent of likely voters now support a temporary halt on Muslim entry into the United States. Just 42 percent of likely voters oppose a temporary ban.   That suggested policy is a much harder line than the temporary ban on Muslim immigration, or the   ban on immigration from countries with a history of radical Islam, that have been proposed by GOP candidate Donald Trump.
