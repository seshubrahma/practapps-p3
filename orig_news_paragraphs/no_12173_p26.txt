The Pew Research analysis finds that, in percentage terms, the bursting of the housing market bubble in 2006 and the recession that followed from late 2007 to   took a far greater toll on the wealth of minorities than whites. From 2005 to 2009, inflation adjusted median wealth fell by 66% among Hispanic households and 53% among black households, compared with just 16% among white households.   As a result of these declines, the typical black household had just $5, 677 in wealth (assets minus debts) in 2009 the typical Hispanic household had $6, 325 in wealth and the typical white household had $113, 149.
