Wage growth is tepid, and the employment to population ratio for people 25 to 54 was 78. 2 percent in December. The Fed’s preferred measure of price inflation, the Bureau of Economic Analysis’ index of personal consumption expenditures, rose by 1. 6 percent in 2016, the strongest performance in more than two years. But inflation remains below the Fed’s goal of a 2 percent annual pace   —   a goal the Fed has not achieved since 2011.
