Yet 140 miles of   roads and an international border divide the two cities, keeping them farther apart than their geographic and cultural identities would suggest. Now the political, academic and tech elite of both cities are looking for ways to bring them closer together, with the aim of continuing the growth of two of the most vibrant economies in North America. " Vancouver has a lot more in common with Seattle than we do with Calgary, Montreal, Toronto, anywhere else in our country," Christy Clark, the premier of British Columbia, said in an interview.
