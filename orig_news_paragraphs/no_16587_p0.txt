 (CNN) Standing in front of a row of American flags and white pillars resembling those at the White House, Hillary Clinton touted support from national security leaders, reminded the country of her role in killing Osama bin Laden, and chastised    with a touch of theatrical flare    her Republican opponent as clueless and entirely out of his element.   With this one brief press conference, Clinton signaled loud and clear: I’ve done this before. Just two months out from Election Day with the race between Clinton and Donald Trump tightening, the former secretary of state’s meeting on Friday with a bipartisan group of foreign policy experts and the press conference that followed demonstrated the increasing prominence of national security in the 2016 campaign.
