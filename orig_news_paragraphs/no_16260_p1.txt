Gloria Steinem: Now it’s up to us, As a teenager, I volunteered for Adlai Stevenson, and was told to hide in another room if the candidate happened into our campaign office, because he was that unacceptable thing, divorced, and couldn’t be seen with women who, of course, could only be perceived in one way.   As a   activist, I learned to campaign with movements outside various presidential campaigns, since those campaigns rarely knew how to talk about issues of gender, race or both, even when candidates were representing those for whom such issues were key    a national majority    better than was their opposition.
