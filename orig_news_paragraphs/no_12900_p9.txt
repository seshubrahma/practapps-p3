The State Department is now estimating there are about 2, 700 pages of schedules left. Under its process, it is reviewing and censoring them    to remove personal details such as private phone numbers or email addresses. In some cases it has censored names of people who met privately with Clinton or the subjects they discussed. A State Department spokeswoman, Elizabeth Trudeau, declined to discuss the ongoing case and noted the agency is struggling with thousands of public records requests.
