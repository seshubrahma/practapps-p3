Constitutional and statutory arguments, Moving to the merits, the states make a variety of statutory and constitutional arguments. Washington and Minnesota argue that the executive orders "target individuals for discriminatory treatment based on their country of origin   religion, without lawful justification" and they say the order was "motivated by animus and a desire to harm a particular group. " They also say that the order violates the Establishment Clause that prohibits the federal government from preferring one religion over another.
