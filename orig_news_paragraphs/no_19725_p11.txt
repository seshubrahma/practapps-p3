Within the last year, several serviceman have sued the Army to be able to keep their turbans and beards. Until now, the Army has responded with temporary fixes —  exemptions granted on a limited,    basis. The military expressed safety concerns about the way beards or other headwear might undermine the effectiveness of gear, and began conducting studies about the feasibility of broader exemptions. Over time, these exemptions provided evidence that soldiers could successfully serve while wearing   dress, according to Fanning’s letter.
