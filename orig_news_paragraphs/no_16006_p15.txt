The immediate stock market contagion unleashed by the referendum across the globe represented the worst equity carnage since the start of the Great Recession in 2008. If the losses prove   the impact of the referendum on the U. S. economy and politics could be temporary. But if "Brexit" ushers in a period of economic volatility across Europe that begins to squelch growth, the U. S. economy could be badly affected    complicating Clinton’s bid to pull off the tough assignment of winning a third consecutive White House term for the Democrats.
