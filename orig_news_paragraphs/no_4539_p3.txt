Cromwell, who altered the course of the Western world in ways still being felt today, was Steve Bannon in feathered Tudor finery.    …     Readers of Hilary Mantel’s revisionist novels, and viewers of the BBC series based on her work, know Thomas Cromwell as a brooding, brilliant master of the court of King Henry VIII, from 1532 to 1540. The real Cromwell was a cunning conspirator who tore up the old order in service of a     king who forced a breakaway religion on his subjects.
