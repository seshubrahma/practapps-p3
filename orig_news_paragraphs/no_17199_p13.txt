If you want to know how Assad is likely to deal with his defeated opponents, just look at the case of the February 1982 Hama uprising.   That year, members of the Muslim Brotherhood tried to seize the central Syrian city from the regime of Hafez   Bashar’s father. Regime forces, led by Bashar’s uncle, Rifaat, surrounded Hama and pummeled it for weeks with tank and artillery fire, killing at least 10, 000. Afterward, they went in, slaughtering thousands more, rebels and civilians alike.
