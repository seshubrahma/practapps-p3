The participants were asked to rate the photos based on hireability, but they also were told that the men and women in the photos were all equally qualified for various jobs. The researchers discovered that there was no significant difference between the ratings of the original and "heavier" photos for male faces. However, there was a statistically significant difference between the ratings of the original and "heavier" photos for female faces, if they were being considered for a job that required a lot of interaction with customers. In other words, the "heavier" women were evaluated significantly more negatively in   job roles, the study suggests.
