Washington (CNN) Even as US diplomats were strongly urging Argentina’s military to improve its human rights record during the country’s   Dirty War, a top White House official argued against strongly punishing the junta for its crackdown on dissidents. President Jimmy Carter’s national security adviser, Zbigniew Brzezinski, cited concerns that any punitive action would hinder the administration’s efforts to secure the support of American conservatives and moderates on other issues, according to newly declassified documents. The Office of the Director of National Intelligence published Monday a trove of over 1, 000 documents that detail America’s relationship with Argentina during the 1970s and ’80s, highlighting US efforts to curb the ruling Argentine junta
