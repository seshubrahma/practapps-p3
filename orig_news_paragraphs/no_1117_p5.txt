No one is following Britain out of the E. U.," Pierpaolo Barbieri wrote recently for Foreign Affairs, a magazine published by the Council on Foreign Relations, a nonpartisan research group. The question now is whether some Europeans, having watched the first aftereffects of the vote to withdraw and the American presidential vote  —   political division in Britain and the fall of the pound, and political missteps in the Trump White House   —   are sobered by the chaos of the right. That thesis is speculative, too, and will be tested next month in France, where the traditional parties have imploded and the   nationalist Marine Le Pen, if victorious, has promised to take France out of the European Union.
