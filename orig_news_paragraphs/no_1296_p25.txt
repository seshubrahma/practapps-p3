In the 1994 issue of The American Historical Review that featured Alan Brinkley’s "The Problem of American Conservatism," Ribuffo wrote a response contesting Brinkley’s contention, now commonplace, that Trilling was right about American conservatism’s shallow roots. Ribuffo argued that America’s   traditions were far more deeply rooted in the past, and far angrier, than most historians would acknowledge, citing a long list of examples from "regional suspicions of various metropolitan centers and the snobs who lived there" to "white racism institutionalized in slavery and segregation.
