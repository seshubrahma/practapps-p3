The Tea Party movement had begun earlier that year, in opposition to President Barack Obama’s stimulus, which sent nearly a trillion dollars to pet projects and state and local governments that had little prospect of creating new jobs. But the people showing up at town hall meetings were not there simply to protest or "resist," though Democrats accused them of "carrying swastikas" and derided them as "Brooks Brothers" demonstrators. Most were not aligned with the Republican Party.
