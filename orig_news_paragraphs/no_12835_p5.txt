What isn’t clear, however, is if Trump’s lead is slipping enough to cause on upset in voting that is just a few days away. Polling in primaries is always difficult, because voters are choosing between candidates when they may have genuinely divided feelings. Most of the candidates, being of the same party, will have at least some appeal to voters. This difficulty is compounded in a state like New Hampshire, where voters have historically waited until the last possible moment to decide on a candidate.
