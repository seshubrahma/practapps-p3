One passage in that volume considers how individuals might optimize their own production and consumption: It is the maxim of every prudent master of a family never to attempt to make at home what it will cost him more to make than to buy. Smith is right, of course everyone should always be calculating, however informally, whether or not it’s cheaper to make it at home or buy it from someone else. We can quickly see: If each family must make its own clothes and grow its own food, it’s likely to be worse off than if it can buy its necessities from a   producer. Why?
