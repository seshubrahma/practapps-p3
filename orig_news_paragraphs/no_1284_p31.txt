The "problem" came two days in, crippling the command module’s supply of electricity, light and water. The   crew was able to make a series of decisions and improvised repairs to the carbon dioxide removal system, enabling them to use the lunar module as a "lifeboat" to safely return to Earth on April 17. Although the mission was aborted and the crew never landed on the moon, their heroics  —   and their rescue  —   resulted in the mission being classified as a "successful failure. "
