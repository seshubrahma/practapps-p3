Home and homecoming were a theme at Billy Reid’s show Monday evening, held in the Cellar of the Beekman hotel, a chic   nestled in the financial district, whose tall structures and windy streets on a cold winter night evoked the "cathedral of Januaries" Frank O’Hara wrote about in "Avenue A. " The moody, elliptical O’Hara poem was recited at the show by the Tony   actor Alex Sharp to a room full of guests eager to welcome Mr. Reid on his return to the New York fashion fold. " It’s good to be here," the designer said to the crowd, referring to a period when he sat out the show cycle.
