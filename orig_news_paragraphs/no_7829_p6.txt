But during the same period as Sculley began his deep foray into politics, Apple’s corporate infrastructure costs ballooned and its products became increasingly commoditized. With Apple’s earnings beginning a historic collapse, Sculley was forced to resign in April 1993. A few years later, Apple was forced to buy Steve Job’s Next Computer   to recruit him and his disruptive product   style back into the company. Over the next 14 years, Jobs launched disruptive products that dominated Silicon Valley innovation, including the iMac in 1998 Power Mac G4 Cube in 2000 iPod in 2001 Mac OS X and Apple Stores in 2001 iTunes Store in 2003
