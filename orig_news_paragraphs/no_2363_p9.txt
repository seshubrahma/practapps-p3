For all the praise for Mrs. Clinton’s performance in the first debate, her surgical takedown of Mr. Trump did little to change voters’ perceptions of her. But, in contrast to Mr. Trump, who prefers big rallies, Mrs. Clinton has interacted regularly with voters during her campaign, and some of her most powerful moments have come in those settings. At a town   event in Iowa, Mrs. Clinton became teary and embraced a    girl who said she had been bullied at school. And just before the New Hampshire primary, in response to a question from a rabbi, Mrs. Clinton delivered an extended    riff about her own spiritual journey.
