And yet in many ways, the 2016 election has served as a rematch between two boomer factions: one candidate promising a   throwback to an idealized postwar America at its most muscular, the other sounding the familiar liberal call to tear down whatever societal barriers still stand. Mrs. Clinton is a long way from the 1969 commencement speaker who told her Wellesley classmates that the challenge for their generation was to "practice politics as the art of making what appears to be impossible, possible. " She is now more likely to warn of the perils of overpromising.
