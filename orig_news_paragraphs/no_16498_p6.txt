In 1980, the year the term became popular, Reagan’s campaign was terrified that President Carter would announce a deal had been reached in the Iran hostage crisis, bolstering Carter’s   prospects.   But the October Surprise has usually turned out to be a bust, a conspiracy imagined. This year, the October Data Dump, rather than the October Surprise, is what both candidates should fear most. It’s possible to imagine a   revelation that leaves Clinton officials struggling to protect their position or Trump facing a defeat even worse than imagined.
