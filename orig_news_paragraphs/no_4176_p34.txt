Marx could see that unchecked and unbalanced   capitalism would quickly pulverize cultures, traditions, even whole nations in an endless gale of creative destruction.   That is, individuals might be better off in some material ways, but, as a community, they would be atomized and unhinged.   In the meantime, Marx continued, left to their own devices, untrammeled free markets would  concentrate most of the wealth in the hands of speculators and other financialists.   And so a result, Marx concluded, the masses, in their vexation, would be ready to experiment with socialism and then communism.
