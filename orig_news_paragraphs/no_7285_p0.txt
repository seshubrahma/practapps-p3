Fidel Castro, the dictator who used firing squads, labor camps, beatings, torture, and hunger to oppress his people for more than half a century, died Friday night at the age of 90. His demise  —   though his brother, Raúl, remains in power   —   has led many to ask what the future holds for Cuba’s   dissident community.
