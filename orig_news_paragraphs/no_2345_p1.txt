Syrian state television reported that troops loyal to the government of President Bashar   had captured the   neighborhood of Farafra, near the city ’s medieval citadel. The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, a monitoring organization based in London, confirmed that government forces had pushed into the center of Aleppo as part of their siege of   areas in the eastern part of what was once Syria’s commercial capital. Airstrikes on Tuesday afternoon killed at least 23 people, including nine children, in the Sha’ar and Mashhad neighborhoods in eastern Aleppo, the observatory reported.
