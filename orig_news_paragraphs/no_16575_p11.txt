US presidents and vice presidents are often welcomed by senior level officials upon their arrival, typically heads of state, government or foreign ministers. But during an August visit to Turkey, Vice President Joe Biden was greeted by the deputy mayor of Ankara. That visit came amid heightened tensions over Ankara’s push for the extradition of   cleric Fethullah Gulen, under suspicion in Turkey for helping plot a failed July coup, as well as US backing for Syrian Kurdish groups fighting ISIS that Turkey considers to be terrorist organizations.
