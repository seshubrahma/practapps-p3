They are also much more likely to routinely handle large caseloads of contraceptive clients, to get women in for appointments in a timely manner, and to offer evening and weekend appointments. And in some communities, Planned Parenthood is the sole source of publicly funded contraceptive care.    In short, it is simply not feasible for other safety net providers that are often already stretched thin to quickly step up and provide timely,   contraceptive care to the many women who might suddenly be unable to obtain care if their local Planned Parenthood has been shut down.
