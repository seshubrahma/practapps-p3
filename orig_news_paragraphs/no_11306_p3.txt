As the Republicans headed to the Cow Palace in San Francisco for their nominating convention, the   forces ramped up their effort. On the eve of the GOP convention, Gov. William Scranton of Pennsylvania mounted an   bid to wrest the nomination from Goldwater, issuing an   broadside that contended "Goldwaterism has come to stand for a whole   collection of absurd and dangerous positions. " Although Scranton took full responsibility for it, his operatives supposedly wrote the letter without his knowledge or approval.
