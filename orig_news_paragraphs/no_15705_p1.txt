They also expect that Trump will demand US fugitives of justice, such as Assata Shakur, who received political asylum on the island after being convicted of killing a New Jersey state trooper and escaping from a US prison, be extradited. And they believe the President will bar American companies from making deals with the Cuban military, which controls much of the   tourism industry. Trump is not expected to reverse all of Obama’s changes, which were seen as the most significant relaxation of tensions between the United States and Cuba since Fidel Castro’s 1959 revolution. But, current and former US officials say, Trump is looking to make a symbolic gesture that will fulfill his campaign promises to conservative   voters and   members of Congress without closing the door on Cuba’s emerging market for US businesses.
