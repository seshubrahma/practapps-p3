While Hispanic turnout this year is largely driven by    voter outreach, Mrs. Clinton has also paid careful attention to select the right issues. And there are few issues that have bonded her as closely to Latinos as immigration. On the polarizing questions surrounding what to do with undocumented immigrants, Mrs. Clinton has taken a more sympathetic posture than many in her party have, including the president, whom she has criticized as acting too aggressively on deportations. She has embraced undocumented immigrants in a way that would have been practically unthinkable when her husband ran for president in 1992.
