The Wrigleyville and Streeterville neighborhoods in Chicago were once mostly slums and derelict warehouses now they are thriving bohemias, home to Starbucks and Trader Joes.   And on the West Coast, the renaissance of downtown Los Angeles —  back in dreary 1981, the location of the dystopic film Blade Runner —  has been a marvel, as have the comebacks of   San Francisco, Portland, and Seattle.
