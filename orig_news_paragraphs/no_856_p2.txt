The political pieces she later wrote for The New York Review of Books   —   beginning with the 1988 presidential campaign, on through the impeachment of Bill Clinton and the high drama of the 2000   in Florida  —   were less original, less idiosyncratic, but reading them in retrospect, they are oddly prophetic about the growing gap between the electorate and the political elites, and the growing dysfunction of the entire system.
