All of the new merchandise has been "proudly made in the USA," according to Trump’s website. Trump has been criticized in the past for outsourcing his own company’s manufacturing jobs to businesses overseas. The campaign relied heavily on merchandise as part of its paid advertising strategy during the primaries before it started rolling out its first general election television ads on Tuesday. It spent $208, 000 on its "Make America Great Again" hats in June in comparison to $120, 000 on online and print ads in the same month.
