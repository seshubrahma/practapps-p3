The third night featured the discordant notes of Cruz displaying the Republican party’s utter lack of unity. Cruz was followed by vice presidential nominee Mike Pence making his case for core conservative values, at a time when Democrats are trying to use Pence’s conservative advocacy on social issues as strike against the Trump ticket for women. But the Trump campaign believes that female voters are more closely aligned with Trump on national security issues and his approach to the economy than they are with Clinton    and they see an opportunity to win them over with his speech and in the campaign this fall.   In coming weeks, Trump’s campaign plans to put him in more intimate settings talking to women about the economy, national security and education.
