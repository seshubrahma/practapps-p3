What’s at stake? This is not the Trump administration ’s first legal showdown over the President’s travel ban.   The original executive order was swiftly blocked nationwide by a federal judge in Seattle, the 9th US Circuit Court of Appeals refused to reinstate it, and then the administration spent over a month rewriting it, exempting several categories of foreign travelers and taking care to make no express mention of religion    all in the hopes of avoiding legal scrutiny for a second time around.
