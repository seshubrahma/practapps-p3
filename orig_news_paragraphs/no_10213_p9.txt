But the Ninth Circuit is so much larger than all the other federal appeals courts   —   covering eleven states, instead of the typical three or four, and with a correspondingly higher number of judges   —   that when the Ninth Circuit takes the rare step of rehearing a case en banc, it means the case is assigned to an   panel, displacing the original   panel.
