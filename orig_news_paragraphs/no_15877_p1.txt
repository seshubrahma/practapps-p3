Top states for overall child   were New Hampshire, Massachusetts, Vermont and Minnesota, with Iowa and Connecticut tied for fifth place. The five worst states for overall child   were Arizona, Nevada, Louisiana, New Mexico and, at the bottom of the list for the second year in a row, Mississippi. The report also pointed out national findings such as how the number of families living in   neighborhoods has risen slightly over the past decade. In Arizona, Louisiana, Mississippi and New Mexico, at least 20% of children lived in   neighborhoods in 2015 as was the case in 2010 to 2014, according to the report.
