Each confronts enormously difficult and relevant issues: racism, homophobia, the generational cycle of violence, the erasure of women of color from our nation’s historical narrative.     Compared to these works, "La La Land" is what its title promises: A slight and slightly nonsensical love letter from Hollywood to itself   —   or, even more bluntly, an acrobatic kissing of its own   ass.    And the fact is, Hollywood doesn’t deserve it.
