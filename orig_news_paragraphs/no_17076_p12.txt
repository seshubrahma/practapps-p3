For too long, Republican presidents have gone for the "stealth" pick, nominations of jurists with little or no judicial track record, who may or may not adhere to a conservative judicial philosophy. Meanwhile, Democratic presidents have nominated men and women who are reliable liberals.   To put it in perspective, former Justice David Souter was appointed by George H. W.
