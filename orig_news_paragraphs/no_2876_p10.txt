In a settlement over the illegal accounts, Wells paid $185 million  —   including $100 million to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, the largest fine that agency had ever assessed. The regulators cited specific technical deficiencies in Wells’s living will, including issues of "shared services" and "legal entity rationalization. " These problems generally point to the fact, regulators have said, that Wells has not figured out how to adequately unwind all of the many complex and interconnected parts of its banking empire.
