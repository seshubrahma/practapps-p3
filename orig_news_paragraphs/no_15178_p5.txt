, There’s going to be a runoff for a Georgia congressional seat in a race seen by some as a referendum on President Trump’s popularity. Democrat Jon Ossoff almost pulled off a stunner, finishing well ahead of 11 GOP challengers in yesterday’s special election but falling just short of the percentage he needed to win outright. The seat, in a Republican stronghold in Atlanta’s northern suburbs, has been held by the GOP since the 1970s. Ossoff will likely face an uphill climb in the June runoff, since it’s expected conservative voters will rally to the   finisher, Republican Karen Handel.
