Since President Xi Jinping launched One Belt, One Road in 2013 many countries in Europe and Asia —  and most recently, Canada —  have signed up to participate in this giant investment and infrastructure program.   Obama administration, however, reacted with indifference and, behind the scenes, even hostility. Many policymakers in the U. S. viewed it as either a giant, wasteful boondoggle or a threat to American leadership. Japan also refused to play along with One Belt, One Road.
