All the largest American banks must submit to regulators their living wills, or strategies for unwinding themselves in an orderly way   —   something they lacked in 2008, when the federal government had to prop up sagging banks like Citigroup and Bank of America. The   requirement, passed as part of the 2010   financial overhaul, is meant to prevent taxpayers from having to bail out big banks again in the event of a huge financial failure. Of all the rules passed in the wake of the crisis, this requirement is designed to address most directly the issue of big banks’ being too big to fail.
