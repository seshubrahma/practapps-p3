The Dow has risen from less than 18, 000 points on Election Day to a peak of more than 21, 000. It has since fallen below that marker, but it’s clear that Wall Street likes what it hears from Trump    both his promises to roll back regulations on businesses and his promise to reform the tax rate.   That’s good news for anyone in the market    and with so many  Americans reliant on IRAs and 401Ks to fund their retirement plans, that’s a large portion of the population. A little more than half of Americans    52%    said they invest in the stock market, according to a Gallup survey in April 2016.
