Despite the robust hiring in the last couple of years, tens of millions of workers have barely felt the benefits of the recovery, and notable pockets of economic weakness remain more than seven years after it began. Those two contrasting realities  —   healthy hiring and falling unemployment on the one hand , millions of economically sidelined Americans on the other  —   sustain the narrative of the two main presidential candidates, Hillary Clinton and Donald J. Trump. Both candidates’ critiques of the economy contain kernels of truth.
