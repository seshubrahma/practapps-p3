Similarly, as of July of this summer, the Washington Post’s Fact Checker column had rated 65% of Trump’s statements as "Four Pinocchios" or "whoppers "  —   meaning, not just false, but flagrantly so.   For comparison, the Post’s Glenn Kessler wrote, "Most politicians tend to earn Four Pinocchios 10 to 20 percent of the time."    In other words, for a guy who has built his campaign in large part on insisting that voters can’t trust normal politicians, Donald Trump has proven himself to be repeatedly untrustworthy
