The case in question involved a felon charged with possessing a gun in violation of a statute which prohibits the "knowing" possession of a firearm by a felon. Gorsuch, in his opinion, reminds us that plenty of minor crimes may technically involve felony convictions even if no jail time is included, so that the convicted defendant may not know he was guilty of a felony.
