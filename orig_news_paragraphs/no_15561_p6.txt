Last week began with former acting Attorney General Sally Yates testifying under oath on May 8 that the White House waited 18 days to fire former national security adviser Michael Flynn after learning he was vulnerable to Russian blackmail. A rocky 10 days, In the days that followed, Trump fired FBI Director James Comey, admitted in an NBC interview that he did so with the Russia probe in mind, and appeared to threaten Comey on Twitter with the prospect that he had tapes of their conversations.   This was before reports revealed that Trump apparently pushed Comey to drop his investigation into Flynn    which critics have painted as an attempt to obstruct justice    and before news broke that the President had apparently revealed classified information to Russian officials.
