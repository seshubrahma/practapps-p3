That is, it’s politically dangerous, often fatal, to pivot from a critique of governmental excess to a critique of basic   programs such as Social Security and Medicare —   and yet that’s exactly what Hudgins does. To be sure, Hudgins has plenty of fellow believers on his side. One can read, for example, the manifestos of Ayn Rand, Ludwig von Mises, or Murray Rothbard and learn the maximum case against even a minimum government safety net.
