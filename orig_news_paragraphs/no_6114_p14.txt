As the wise say in DC, The President proposes, and the Congress disposes. Of course, in the real world, any president possesses enormous power.   And yet as the famed political scientist Richard Neustadt put it, The power of the presidency is the power to persuade  that is, the President can use the "bully pulpit" to set the agenda and advance his program. The phrase "bully pulpit," of course, comes from our 26th president, Theodore Roosevelt he knew how to get his way.
