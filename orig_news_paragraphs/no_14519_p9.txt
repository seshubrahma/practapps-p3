Contrary to   predictions concerning the country’s impending collapse, however, it seems that the paradox of the North Korean regime is not its instability but its resilience. Even during the time of the famine of the 1990s, long before North Korea’s mineral export boom, there was a distinct lack of any overt actions of resistance amongst the North Korean populace. In addition, sanctions are widely regarded as ineffectual if there are no existing splits amongst elites or opposition movements within the country. North Korea is notably lacking in either.
