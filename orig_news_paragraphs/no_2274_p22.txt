Mr. Vance, a conservative, takes a far dimmer view. Whether you agree with Mr. Vance or not, you must admire him for his   confrontation with a taboo subject. And he frames his critique generously, stipulating that it isn’t laziness that’s destroying hillbilly culture but what the psychologist Martin Seligman calls "learned helplessness"  —   the fatalistic belief, born of too much adversity, that nothing can be done to change your lot. What he’s really writing about is despair.
