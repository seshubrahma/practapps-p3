If such clueless condescension is a key part of Establishmentist thinking, we can also note a more brutal aspect: the process of bludgeoning the lower orders into submission. Yes, this is an old story: the Establishment hiring courtiers and henchmen, tasking them with keeping the peasants quiescent. We might dub these henchmen —  and, to be fair, henchwomen —  as Compradores. That’s a Portuguese word for middleman, which historians have used to lump together all the   agents of old colonialism.
