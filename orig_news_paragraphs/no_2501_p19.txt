While Mr. Trump has yet to specify how he would renegotiate Nafta, his emphasis has been on increasing American jobs and discouraging automakers from using foreign plants to supply the American market. His proposal to put tariffs of as much as 35 percent on vehicles imported from Mexico has stunned auto executives who have built their business models on open borders for cars, trucks and the thousands of parts in them. " A tariff like that would be imposed on the entire auto sector, and that could have a huge impact on the U. S. economy," said Mark Fields, chief executive of Ford.
