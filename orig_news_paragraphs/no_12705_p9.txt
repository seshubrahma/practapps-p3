Asked whether Muslims can be "full citizens" in France, Le Pen said while she believes France is "full up" and cannot absorb more waves of migrants , she doesn’t judge people on their religion and is far more concerned over whether citizens value the rule of law and secularism in the country. Affirming her personal commitment to the separation of church and state, and describing it as "part of France’s identity" the Front National leader said her party judges people on their respect for French values but "never based on their religion".
