Sulaimani, Iraq (CNN) In a tea room in Sulaimani’s old bazaar that’s dense with the smoke of scores of cigarettes, bunches of grizzled,   men    some wearing traditional Kurdish baggy trousers, other wearing suits without ties    are arguing furiously about the great question of the day:   What happens to Iraq after ISIS loses the key Iraqi city of Mosul? For the moment, the Iraqi army, Kurdish peshmerga forces, Shia militias and Sunni tribal units are all united in fighting ISIS.
