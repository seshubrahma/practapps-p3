As the largest Shiite nation in the world, Iran forged a natural alliance with Assad and his father, Syrian head of state Hafez   both Alawite Shiite Muslims. As a longtime ally of the regime in Tehran, the terrorist organization Hezbollah has also involved itself in Syria, aiding the Assad military. Hezbollah has not only vowed to defend Assad, but to "continue to bear our great responsibilities of jihad there," according to leader Hassan Nasrallah. Iran, meanwhile, has declared Syria part of its growing empire.
