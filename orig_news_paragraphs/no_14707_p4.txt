Conspiracy theories have always been part of American politics. The historian Richard Hofstadter famously made this a central part of the study of American politics. He best captured this thesis in his 1964 Harper’s Magazine article, "The Paranoid Style in American Politics," where he wrote about the ways in which conspiracy theories had been used by various political movements throughout U. S. history.
