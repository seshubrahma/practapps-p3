Trump has continued to speak of a "ban," even though the label could hurt his legal case. More egregious were Trump’s attempts to influence FBI Director James Comey, who was investigating his election campaign’s possible ties to Russia. Trump took the risky step of firing Comey, and then told Russian officials in the Oval Office that he considered the director to be "crazy" and a "nut job."   Not surprisingly, Comey is now willing to testify to a Senate committee about a controversy that raises the specter of a President seeking to obstruct justice, which many consider an impeachable offense.
