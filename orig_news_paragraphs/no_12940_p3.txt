In both 2008 and 2012, Republican voters who identified themselves as "very conservative" made up roughly 35 percent of the electorate. In this poll, they are just 27 percent of the sample. That said, Trump’s lead is so large that even if "very conservative" voters turned out in historic numbers, it wouldn’t likely tip the balance that much based on current trends. It is important to keep in mind that the poll was conducted from Wednesday to Friday, before Saturday’s Republican debate in Charleston.
