Led by philosopher Nick Land and computer scientist Curtis Yarvin, this group began a gleeful demolition of the   biases of western political discourse. Liberalism, democracy and egalitarianism were all put under the microscope of the neoreactionaries, who found them wanting. Liberal democracy, they argued, had no better a historical track record than monarchy, while egalitarianism flew in the face of every piece of research on hereditary intelligence. Asking people to see each other as human beings rather than members of a demographic   meanwhile, ignored every piece of research on tribal psychology.
