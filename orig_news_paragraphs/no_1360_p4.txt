The ties between Republican elites  —   elected officials, donors and Washington insiders  —   and voters have actually been fraying for years. Traditional power brokers long preached   conservatism and wanted to pursue an immigration overhaul, entitlement cuts, free trade and a hawkish foreign policy, and nominees like John McCain and Mitt Romney largely embraced that agenda.
