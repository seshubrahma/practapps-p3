Seeing that     conservatism was destined to fail in the face of industrialization, urbanization, and proletarianization, Disraeli picked up his pen he wrote not only political pamphlets, but also novels that mixed   intrigue with a reformist message. Elected to Parliament in 1837, Disraeli articulated his One Nation idea, championing policies —  and more to the point, an overall approach —  that he believed would not only quell revolution but also bring the English working class into the Tory fold. Disraeli described English workingmen as "angels in marble.
