At a Senate confirmation hearing, Mr. Carson, 65, strained at times to square his past remarks on the dangers of federal assistance   —    he once called poverty "really more of a choice than anything else"  —   with the mission of the Department of Housing and Urban Development, an agency with a $47 billion budget and a mandate to help millions of   renters and struggling homeowners. Forgoing many specifics, he laid out a vision of a more "holistic" approach: recruiting private sector dollars and seeking to end what he called a cycle of "generation after generation of people living in dependent situations.
