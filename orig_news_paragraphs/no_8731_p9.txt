That is not the role the Constitution assigns to federal courts. In Article III, the Supreme Law of Land confines the "judicial power" to deciding cases or controversies in which (1) the plaintiff has suffered a distinct and concrete personal injury (separate from society at large) (2) that can be fairly traced to the defendant, and (3) which the court can remedy if it grants the requested relief.
