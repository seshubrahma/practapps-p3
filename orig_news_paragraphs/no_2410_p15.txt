In his new book, the social critic Thomas Frank poses another possibility: that liberals in general  —   and the Democratic Party in particular   —   should look inward to understand the sorry state of American politics. Too busy attending TED talks and vacationing in Martha’s Vineyard, Frank argues, the Democratic elite has abandoned the party’s traditional commitments to the working class. In the process, they have helped to create the political despair and anger at the heart of today’s   insurgencies.
