Doors at many Macy’s, Sears and J. C. Penney stores may still be open, but some of the jobs they once supported are starting to vanish. General merchandise stores shed 34, 700 jobs in March, the government announced Friday, the single most disappointing figure in a generally disappointing jobs report. After hitting a low point during the recession in December 2009, the retail sector has reliably been churning out more jobs.
