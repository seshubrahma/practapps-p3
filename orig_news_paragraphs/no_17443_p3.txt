They look at the growing economic divide between the rich and the poor, fearing that they and their children will fall down the bottom rung of the latter.   They are doing much better in terms of employment and income than they were a few years ago, but the kinds of jobs they enjoy seem less stable than in the heyday of unions, with fewer benefits and worse prospects for upward mobility. With rising education and health care costs, they worry about how their kids will do. This issue animated the supporters of Bernie Sanders in the Democratic primary.
