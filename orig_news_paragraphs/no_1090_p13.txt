States often have different names for the program, and many who rely on it don’t realize that MassHealth in Massachusetts or TennCare in Tennessee are just Medicaid by another name. But in Kaiser’s polling since 2005, the percentage of people who support cutting Medicaid spending has never exceeded 13 percent. " The conventional wisdom that there’s a great deal of stigma attached to this program does not bear out in the public opinion data," said Mollyann Brodie, who oversees polling for the foundation.
