Yet despite all the calls for unity, the prayers offered and the platitudes voiced, there are few signs yet that any candidate, lawmaker, law enforcement leader, or civil rights campaigner has mustered the trust and respect of voters needed to knit a mournful nation together and freshen the stale national debate on race and violence.   The leadership vacuum raises questions about whether there will ever be a narrowing of the estrangement between   communities and police. It raises doubts that there will ever be room for compromise between   advocates and Second Amendment absolutists or whether the criminal justice reform both Republicans and Democrats say they support will ever transit a Congress consumed by its own rancor.   The political scripts on Friday were certainly familiar after the tragedy in Dallas, which fused two festering issues that divide the nation    easy access to firearms and discord over race    into a night of terrible mayhem.
