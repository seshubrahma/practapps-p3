Austria, Like the Netherlands, Austria rejected its   candidate, bringing a wave of relief to EU leaders in Brussels, who are trying to stave off growing discontent with the union from across the content.   Austria looked at electing the EU’s first   head of state since World War II when it picked its next president on the same day as Italy’s critical constitutional reforms ballot in December.   Norbert Hofer of the Freedom Party of Austria (FPO) had ridden a populist wave to challenge for presidential power (albeit in a largely ceremonial role). The race had appeared close, but Hofer conceded to   independent Alexander Van der Bellen, when early returns ran against him.
