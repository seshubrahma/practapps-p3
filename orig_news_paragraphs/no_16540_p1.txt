During his speech about immigration, in which  Trump defied predictions that he was about to make his   pivot to moderation, he doubled down on his hardline stance against immigration. Surrounding himself with parents and family whose loved ones had been killed by undocumented "illegal aliens," Trump reiterated his hardline opposition to immigrants and outlined the reasons why he thought President Obama’s and Hillary Clinton’s positions are destructive.    Although he backed away from earlier primary claims that he would deport 12 million illegal immigrants, now saying that only 2 million criminals would be getting the boot, that was not much of a concession. He stood firmly against embracing a path to citizenship and his fierce rhetoric belied any possibility that he was turning some corner.
