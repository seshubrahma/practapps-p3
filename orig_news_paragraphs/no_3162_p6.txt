This sets aside the fact that the independent counsel statute —   the Ethics in Government Act of 1978 —  is unconstitutional, upheld by the Supreme Court in its 1988 case, Morrison v. Olson, over the dissent of Justice Antonin Scalia. But political dramas in subsequent years proved Scalia’s argument correct on why a prosecutor, fully independent from the elected head of the executive branch, is incompatible with the Constitution’s structure of American government. Now, many leading liberal judges agree that while the Constitution allows special counsels, it does not allow independent counsels.
