That       style has helped raise Kasich’s political stock, at least in Ohio. The Republican scored a resounding victory in his 2014   fight, receiving 64% of the vote and winning all but two of the Buckeye State’s 88 counties, albeit against a flawed Democratic challenger. That 2014 victory was even more remarkable considering Kasich’s standing just a few years earlier. Shortly after taking office, Kasich pushed to end the collective bargaining rights for public employee unions in the state, a bruising battle that saw his approval ratings dip into the 30s.
