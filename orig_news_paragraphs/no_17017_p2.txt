Ellison, the first black Muslim elected to Congress, enters the contest with the backing from across the left. He’s won endorsements from Sen. Bernie Sanders, the fiery independent and former Democratic primary candidate, along with outgoing Senate Minority Leader Harry Reid and Sen. Chuck Schumer, who is set to replace Reid in 2017, all pledging their support. But other politicos have expressed interest in the position.   Howard Dean, who ran the Democratic National Committee from 2005 to 2009, and announced last week he would again seek its top role, and is expected to be among Ellison’s most formidable challengers.
