Castro’s   sister Juanita Castro said she would not be leaving exile in Miami to attend the funeral. Nearly every Latin American head of state will be on hand, though. The arrival of   presidents of Bolivia, Ecuador, Nicaragua and Venezuela is no surprise and in fact speaks to how much the world has changed since Fidel Castro took power in 1959. Shortly after the Cuban revolution, every country in the Western Hemisphere except Mexico broke relations with Cuba, alarmed at Castro’s growing alliance with the Soviet Union.
