If "yes" seems impossible but "no" seems insufficient, what fills the space between? Before Trump’s election, anyone who claimed to have been a member of "the resistance" was most likely over the age of 85, a veteran of   struggles in France and other   territories during World War II. That resistance involved armed conflict and personal risk of the bleakest sort, with guerrilla fighters hiding in the catacombs of Paris while Hitler’s forces did their worst above ground. Under fascist rule, there were no plausible options for political engagement.
