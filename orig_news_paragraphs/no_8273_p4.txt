Before the nation went to the polls in October 2015,   Kirchner successor Daniel Scioli appeared poised to take over and continue the left’s  . His conservative opponent, Buenos Aires Mayor Mauricio Macri, forced the first   vote in that nation’s history, however, and easily beat Scioli in November. Among the issues tipping the scales against Scioli were the increasingly sluggish economy  —   a product of strict   price controls   —   and the death of prosecutor Alberto Nisman, found shot dead in his apartment after accusing the government of aiding in covering up Iran’s role in the AMIA bombing.
