Mr. Depp’s situation is so complicated that, in most ways, the federal fiduciary rules don’t apply. And his lawsuit will be unaffected by whatever President Trump proposes, because it has been filed in state court. Nonetheless, his suit is illuminating, because it demonstrates that instead of glibly dismissing the rules’ critics, we ought to acknowledge the challenges they voice. Consider, for instance, the claims made by Mr. Depp’s lawyers that the Management Group never once paid the actor’s taxes on time in 16 years (which the company disputes).
