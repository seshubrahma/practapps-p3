Crowds of more than 10, 000 people came to see the early stars of the sport push the limits of the big machines at more than 90 miles an hour around the banked track, essentially a   version of a bicycle velodrome. The racers included characters like the   Barney Oldfield, the millionaire playboy Caleb Bragg, and a native son, "Terrible" Teddy Tetzlaff. " It was a crazy, great time," said Harold Osmer, a racing historian.
