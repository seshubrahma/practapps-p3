Their anger is understandable. The group is a remnant of a   United Auto Workers union local, which at its peak in the 1960s represented over 5, 000 workers who made transmissions in Muncie for BorgWarner Inc, the global auto parts maker. An American flag drapes the wall at one end of the room, portraits of former labor bosses line the other, salvaged from a union hall that had its own baseball field. For this group of mostly white,   men, the last two decades have brought much loss.
