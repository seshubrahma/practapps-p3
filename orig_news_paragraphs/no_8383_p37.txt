WND used user data from Facebook to track the performance of their own site, as well as a number of conservative competitors including Breitbart, The Washington Examiner, The Daily Caller, The Washington Free Beacon, and The Blaze between January and May 2016. In that   period, the data shows that despite a rise in the number of "likes," or fans of all the tracked conservative sites, the number of engagements (the number of people who see posts, react to, share, or click on a post) went down. Breitbart, for example, had 1. 6 million likes and an engagement rate of 100 percent at the start of 2016.
