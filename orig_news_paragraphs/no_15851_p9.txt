Not too much, except that commonwealths have their own constitutions. Puerto Rican residents have been US citizens since 1917 (thanks to the Jones Act) so they receive many of the same benefits and protections, with just a few differences. For one, they can truthfully say, "Don’t blame me, I didn’t vote for them," when discussing the US presidency. While voters can participate in primary elections, they can’t vote for president in the general election.
