While Levin presents himself as a guardian of our founders’ economic principles, his support for unilateral free trade ironically places his trading views closer to Karl Marx than it does to the legacy of Washington, Hamilton, and Lincoln. In his 1848 address to the Democratic Association of Brussels, Marx declared that he was "in favor of free trade" because of its destructive capabilities for hastening social revolution: The free trade system is destructive.
