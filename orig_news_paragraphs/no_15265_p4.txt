Tax cuts skewed toward the upper income brackets play with well with the business and financial community that have often been big supporters of the GOP.   In addition, shrewd conservatives like David Stockman, who served as President Reagan’s budget director, championed the "starve the beast" argument. It’s a losing argument to convince Americans that government programs which they perceive as providing benefits should be cut. So if the Republicans wanted to shrink government programs, their best bet was to lower tax revenues, making less money available to Washington for spending.
