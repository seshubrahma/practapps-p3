Inevitably, a Palestinian Nelson Mandela would emerge    a symbolic freedom fighter who Israel would have to demonize or imprison. I served as speechwriter to Mandela during his first visit to the United States after his release from prison. He was lionized from New York to Los Angeles and confided in me that he had no doubt his suffering was key to the end of apartheid in his country. But if apartheid was toxic merely to South Africa, a   solution and a globally blacklisted Israel threatens to be toxic to the entire Middle East.
