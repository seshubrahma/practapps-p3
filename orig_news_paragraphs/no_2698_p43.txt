Speculation about a sequel war between Kurdistan and Iraq was (and still is) commonplace. But so far relations were cordial, and the Iraqi military, knowing it would have its hands full with the close combat in Mosul’s ancient streets, was content to leave the Bashiqa campaign to the Kurds. " This war is very different for us Kurds," Barzani’s brother, Sihad Barzani, explained several days after the campaign began.
