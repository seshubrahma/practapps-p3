Her name is Lionel Shriver, and she was clearly out to provoke a reaction, wearing a sombrero throughout her talk (an allusion to a kerfuffle at Bowdoin College last year, where a couple of students were censored for wearing " " to a tequila party).   Shriver, as a novelist, makes several good points. As a writer, she wants to feel free to write about anything she wants. She’s justifiably worried that "the kind of fiction we are ’allowed’ to write is in danger of becoming so hedged, so circumscribed, so   that we’d indeed be better off not writing the anodyne drivel to begin with."
