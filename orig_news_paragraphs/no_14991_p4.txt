The Mosul investigation will undoubtedly consider two principles of the law of war: civilian distinction and proportionality. These mandate that civilians and civilian property may never be intentionally targeted.   If, however, fighters or military objects are targeted and civilians are killed or property destroyed unintentionally, no legal violation has occurred so long as the deaths or destruction are not disproportionate to the value of the military objective.
