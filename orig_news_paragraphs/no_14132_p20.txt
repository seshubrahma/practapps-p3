Based on the wording of the executive order, a criminal could include someone who’s charged with    but not actually convicted    of a crime. The last provisions apparently include anyone who an immigration official feels endangers "public safety or national security," even if that person doesn’t face charges    giving wide latitude to officers.   And the term "criminal offense," isn’t defined in the order and could include a wide range of charges, including presumably misdemeanors.
