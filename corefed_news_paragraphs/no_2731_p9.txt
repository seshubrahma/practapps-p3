In the Justice Department years , the administration won increases for the courts from Congress , and the number of immigration judges rose by 65 to about 300 today .But the Congress of judges is glacially slow .With each judge completing an average of 750 cases a year , the courts would need at least 520 judges to eliminate the backlog within one year , according to the courts from Congress by Human Rights First , a watchdog group in New York .The worst crunch was created by the Obama administration after families from Central America surged across the southwest border in 2014 and again this year .
