The power and presence of Latinos in this community in Orange County   —    itself once a bastion of Republicanism   —    is echoed up and down the California coast .Latinos now make up just under 40 percent of the state ’s population , projected to increase to 47 percent by 2050 .The leaders of both houses of the Legislature are Latino , as is the secretary of state , the current mayor of Los Angeles and the previous mayor .More than 25 percent of Latino voters in the nation live in California , said Mark Hugo Lopez , the director of Hispanic research at the Pew Research Center .
