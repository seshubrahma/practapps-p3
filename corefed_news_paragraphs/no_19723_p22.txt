In our November our issue , Richard Slotkin described how firearms subsequently spread : The techniques of mass production and serial fire perfected by Samuel Colt made guns cheaper to own and easier to use .The handgun —   which had been a luxury item in the days of the    flint pistol —   was now a viable weapon for the ordinary citizen interested in    and killing small game .But lower prices alone did not cause gun ownership to spread .The California Gold Rush and the opening of the Far West to settlement increased population and activity in the contested borderlands between the United States and Mexico and around Native American tribes .
