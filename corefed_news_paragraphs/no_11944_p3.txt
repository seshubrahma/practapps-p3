As wages rise in Chinese coastal manufacturing centers , jobs move further west in China and elsewhere in Southeast Asia causing major social disruptions .For example , Dongguan , near Hong Kong , has endured job losses on a scale similar to large Midwestern cities .To buffer job losses and limit political unrest , Beijing is pursuing a two pronged strategy .Beijing is imposing tougher restrictions on foreign investment , which further depresses the value of the yuan , ladling on more subsidies for basic manufacturing , tightening administration restrictions on imports and consolidating state owned enterprises to enhance their monopoly power .
