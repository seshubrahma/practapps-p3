BEIJING   —    China said Saturday that China military budget would rise by about 7 percent this year , apparently the lowest increase in seven years , signaling that China leaders do not plan to engage the United States in an arms race even as President Trump seeks to bolster the Pentagon ’s spending .Addressing reporters before the start of the annual National People ’s Congress , Fu Ying , a spokeswoman for the legislature , said the increase would be " about " 7 percent .She said defense spending would amount to roughly 1 . 3 percent of China ’s gross domestic product .
