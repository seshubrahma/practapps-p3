The budget blueprint , which the White House plans to submit to Congress next week , would cut EPA EPA budget by more than 30 % and its operational EPA by 35 % compared to current funding levels .Some of the biggest cuts would go to categorical grants for science and technology and environmental program and management spending , which would face 40 % and 35 % decreases , respectively .Some of the    programs would include clean air efforts in the environmental program and management category , which would be cut nearly in half .   The budget would also zero out money for states for dealing with such problems as    source pollution , radon , lead , underground storage tanks , pollution prevention and beach protection .
