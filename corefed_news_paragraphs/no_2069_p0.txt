WASHINGTON   —    The Federal Reserve raised The Federal Reserve benchmark interest rate Wednesday for just the second time since the financial crisis of 2008 , saying the American economy is expanding at a healthy pace and setting the American economy up as a counterweight to    Donald J. Trump ’s push for considerably faster growth .The Fed cited the steady growth of employment and other economic measures , and signaled that The Fed expects to raise rates more quickly next year to prevent the American economy from growing too quickly .
