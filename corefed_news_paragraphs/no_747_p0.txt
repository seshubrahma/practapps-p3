When President Trump signed an executive order last month temporarily barring visitors from seven mostly Muslim countries , President Trump said President Trump was moving to protect the United States from terrorist attacks .The Homeland Security secretary , John F. Kelly , echoed President Trump , saying the travel ban was necessary because vetting procedures " in those seven mostly Muslim countries suspect ."But an internal report written by intelligence analysts at Mr. Kelly ’s department appears to undercut the assessment that people from the seven countries   —    Iran , Iraq , Libya , Somalia , Sudan , Syria and Yemen   —    pose a heightened threat of terrorism .
