The federal government government price controls or engage in direct negotiations over price on most health care products and services .Indeed , in the parts of the health care system where competition thrives , the marketplace maximizes choices for consumers while holding down their costs .   Fortunately consumers policymakers in America have examples of successful programs in the health care sector that have been premised on competition and choice to drive value and innovation .Two , in particular , are of special note     the Medicare Prescription Drug Benefit ( Part D ) and Medicare Advantage plans .
